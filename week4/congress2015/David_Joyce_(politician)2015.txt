'''David Patrick "Dave" Joyce''' (born March 17, 1957) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Ohio's 14th congressional district]] since 2013.<ref>[http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/david-joyce-house-r-ohio-14.html David Joyce, R (Ohio-14) : Roll Call<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> An attorney, Joyce was previously the [[Prosecutor]] of [[Geauga County, Ohio]]. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life and Education==
Dave Joyce was born in [[Cleveland, Ohio]], to a devout Irish [[Roman Catholic|Catholic]] family.{{cn|date=October 2014}} His father was a coal salesman.  In high school, he played football, and considered joining the priesthood.{{cn|date=October 2014}}  In 1975, Joyce enrolled at the [[University of Dayton]], a Catholic university in [[Dayton, Ohio]], from which he received a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in 1979, and later a [[Juris Doctor]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ohio, 14th House District|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress/ohio-14th-house-district-20121101|website=National Journal|publisher=Atlantic Media Company|accessdate=1 July 2014}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2012
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 14}}
In July 2012, U.S. Congressman [[Steve LaTourette]] of [[Ohio's 14th congressional district]] announced that he would be retiring in 2012, rather than seek re-election. Joyce was appointed as the replacement nominee on the Republican ticket.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sherman|first1=Jake|title=Steve LaTourette blames polarizing climate for departure|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0712/79168.html|accessdate=11 July 2014|publisher=Politico|date=2012-07-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Gomez|first1=Henry|title=Northeast Ohio GOP leaders choose David Joyce to replace outgoing Rep. Steve LaTourette on ballot|url=http://www.cleveland.com/open/index.ssf/2012/08/northeast_ohio_gop_leaders_cho.html|accessdate=11 July 2014|publisher=Cleveland.com|date=8-10-2012}}</ref>

Joyce ran in the November general election against perennial [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] candidate [[Dale Virgil Blanchard]], [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] David Macko and [[Green Party (United States)|Green Party]] candidate Elaine Mastromatteo. Joyce won the election with 54% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|title=2012 Ohio House Results|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/results/house/ohio/|accessdate=11 July 2014|publisher=Politico}}</ref>

=== Legislation sponsored ===
In 2014, Joyce introduced the Safe Streets Act with Congresswoman [[Doris Matsui]]. The bill would nationalize transportation "design elements" so that streets would be designed with the safety of drivers, pedestrians, and bicyclists in mind.<ref>Martin, Aaron. [http://riponadvance.com/news/bipartisan-measure-improve-road-safety/6537 "Bipartisan measure would improve road safety"]. ''Ripon Advance''. February 10, 2014. (Retrieved 02-11-14).</ref>

==Personal life==
Dave married Kelly in 1990. They live in [[Russell Township, Geauga County, Ohio|Russell Township]] with their three children: Trenton, KK, and Bridey. He is a member of the [[District attorney|National District Attorney Association]] and the [[Ohio Prosecuting Attorney Association]]. He is also Director of the Geauga Bluecoats Inc.<ref>[http://www.prosecutor.co.geauga.oh.us/TheProsecutor/AboutTheProsecutor.aspx Geauga County Prosecutor - About the Prosecutor<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>
