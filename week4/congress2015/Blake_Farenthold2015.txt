'''Randolph Blake Farenthold'''<ref>[http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2822/Rep_Blake_Farenthold_TX.html Representative Randolph Blake Farenthold (Blake) (R-Texas, 27th) - Biography from LegiStorm<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> (born December 12, 1961) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|TX|27}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life and education==

Farenthold was born and raised in [[Corpus Christi, Texas|Corpus Christi]], Texas, the son of Mary Sue (n√©e Ogg) and George Randolph "Randy" Farenthold. His paternal grandfather, George Edward Farenthold, was a [[Belgian]] immigrant who was the descendant of an aristocratic industrialist family and worked in the oil industry in Texas.<ref>{{cite news|last=Draper|first=Robert|title=The Blood of the Farentholds|url=http://www.texasmonthly.com/content/blood-farentholds/page/0/1|accessdate=20 October 2013|newspaper=Texas Monthly|date=April 1992}}</ref><ref>[http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/farenthold.htm Blake Farenthold ancestry<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Farenthold attended [[Incarnate Word Academy (Corpus Christi, Texas)|Incarnate Word Academy]] and the [[University of Texas at Austin]] where he received a bachelor of science degree in Radio, Television, and Film. He also graduated from [[St. Mary's University School of Law]] in San Antonio.<ref name="Blake Farenthold Campaign Website">[http://blakefarenthold.com/about/ Blake Farenthold Campaign Website], Accessed on November 3, 2010</ref>

==Radio career==
Farenthold's pre-political career includes working as a radio disc jockey in high school and college, seven years of practicing law at the Kleberg Law Firm in [[Corpus Christi, TX|Corpus Christi]] and founding Farenthold Consulting LLC, a computer consulting and web design firm.

Farenthold co-hosted ''Lago in the Morning'', a conservative [[talk radio]] program on [[KKTX (AM)|KKTX]] radio, until he began his political campaign.

Farenthold's radio career is the model for that of Rev. Spotty Spitford, the antagonist in Jacob Appel's (2012) [[satiric]] novel, ''[[The Man Who Wouldn't Stand Up]]''.<ref>Appel, JM.  The Man Who Wouldn't Stand Up, 2012</ref><ref>Wilson, C.  Novel Notes.  ''Spotlight on Fiction''.  6 Jan 2013.</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2010#District 27*}}

Farenthold defeated [[incumbent]] [[Democratic Party (US)|Democratic]] Rep. [[Solomon Ortiz]] by 799 votes on election night.<ref name="Corpus Christi Caller Times">[http://www.caller.com/news/2010/nov/03/farenthold-ousts-ortiz-in-tight-race-n-deck/ Farenthold Ousts Ortiz in Tight Race], Accessed on November 3, 2010</ref>  Ortiz asked for a manual recount. On Monday, November 22, Ortiz conceded the race to Farenthold. Farenthold's final margin of victory over Ortiz was 47.85 to 47.1 percent held.<ref name=TXSOS10>{{cite news | title = 2010 General Election, Election Night Returns, Unofficial Elections Results As Of: 11/3/2010 12:14:58 PM | date = 2010-11-03 | url = http://enr.sos.state.tx.us/enr/results/nov02_154_state.htm?x=0&y=3608&id=179 | work = Texas Secretary of State | accessdate = 2010-11-22}}</ref> His margin of victory was 799 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/03/22/bill-maher-flip-a-district_n_5013348.html |title=Bill Maher's Campaign To Find The Worst Member Of Congress Is Underway|author=Chris Gentilviso|publisher=The Huffington Post|date=23 March 2014|accessdate=January 2, 2015}}</ref> Ortiz had represented the district since its creation in 1982.

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012#District 27}}
[[Redistricting]] after the 2010 census made Farenthold's district significantly more Republican. His old district had been 70 percent Latino, but the new map shifted most of the Latino areas to the newly created 34th district.  To make up for the loss in population, his district was shifted well to the north and east, absorbing some heavily Republican territory near [[Houston, Texas|Houston]] and [[Austin, Texas|Austin]].

He defeated Democratic nominee Rose Meza Harrison 57%-39%.<ref>http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist.exe</ref>

;2014

Farenthold was not challenged in the Republican primary. In the general election, he defeated Democrat Wesley Reed.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Hendricks|first1=Dave|title=Farenthold retains congressional seat|url=http://www.caller.com/news/politics/national/farenthold-takes-commanding-lead-in-early-voting_26305095|accessdate=17 December 2014|work=Corpus Christie Caller Times|date=4 November 2014}}</ref>

===Tenure===
Farenthold has joined the [[Republican Study Committee]], as well as the [[Tea Party Caucus]]. Since redistricting in 2011, his district runs along the middle Texas gulf coast from [[Corpus Christi, Texas|Corpus Christi]] to [[Bay City, Texas|Bay City]] and inland to [[Luling, Texas|Luling]], and includes [[Aransas County, Texas|Aransas]], [[Calhoun County, Texas|Calhoun]], [[Jackson County, Texas|Jackson]], [[Lavaca County, Texas|Lavaca]], [[Matagorda County, Texas|Matagorda]], [[Nueces County, Texas|Nueces]], [[Refugio County, Texas|Refugio]], [[San Patricio County, Texas|San Patricio]], [[Victoria County, Texas|Victoria]], [[Wharton County, Texas|Wharton]], and parts of [[Bastrop County, Texas|Bastrop]], [[Caldwell County, Texas|Caldwell]], and [[Gonzales County, Texas|Gonzales]] counties.

In December 2014, Farenthold was sued by a former staffer, who accused the congressman of gender discrimination, saying that he created a hostile work environment and improperly fired her after she complained.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bresnahan|first1=John|title=Ex-spokeswoman sues Blake Farenthold, alleges discrimination|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2014/12/blake-farenthold-lawsuit-discrimination-113620.html|accessdate=17 December 2014|work=Politico|date=16 December 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Farenthold lives with his wife Debbie and two daughters Morgan and Amanda in [[Corpus Christi, Texas|Corpus Christi]]. He is the step-grandson of [[Sissy Farenthold]], a long-time Democratic icon in Texas, who was married to his grandfather, George Farenthold, from 1950 until 1985.{{citation needed|date=September 2013}}

In 1972, when Farenthold was ten years old, his father disappeared and was later found dead, his body having washed ashore after being weighed down with a cement block and deposited in [[Corpus Christi Bay]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Millionaire slain; Found on beach|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1314&dat=19720607&id=e-0vAAAAIBAJ&sjid=j-wDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3954,2965899|accessdate=20 October 2013|newspaper=The Spokesman-Review|date=7 June 1972|agency=Associated Press|location=Spokane, WA}}</ref> The gangland-style murder was the work of enemies of the elder Farenthold, who feared he would testify against a group of con artists who had tried to defraud him out of $100,000.<ref>{{cite news|last=Draper|first=Robert|title=The Blood of the Farentholds|url=http://www.texasmonthly.com/content/blood-farentholds/page/0/3|accessdate=20 October 2013|newspaper=Texas Monthly|date=April 1992}}</ref>

===Controversy over pajama images===

In 2010, images of Farenthold dressed in duck pajamas alongside women in lingerie emerged on the website thecrushgirls.com. Farenthold's Democratic challenger subsequently ran a political ad highlighting the unusual subject nature of the matter.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Siegel|first1=Elyse|title=GOP Candidate Blake Farenthold Targeted After Being Caught in Ducky Pajamas With Scantily Clad Women|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/10/16/blake-farenthold-pictures_n_764685.html|accessdate=8 December 2014|work=The Huffington Post|date=16 October 2010}}</ref>
