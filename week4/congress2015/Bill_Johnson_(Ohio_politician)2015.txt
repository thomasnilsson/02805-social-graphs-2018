'''William Leslie "Bill" Johnson''' (born November 10, 1954) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OH|6}} since 2011. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and business career==
Born in Roseboro, North Carolina in 1954,<ref name=CQ/> Johnson grew up on family farms.  He entered the [[United States Air Force]] in 1973, married  Wanda Florence Porter April 30, 1975.  They had 3 children, Joshua Daniel Johnson born 1976; Julie Florence Johnson born 1978 and Jessica Leslie Johnson born 1983. He retired as a Lieutenant Colonel after a military career of more than 26 years. He graduated ''summa cum laude'' from [[Troy University]] in [[Troy, Alabama]] in 1979, and he earned his Master’s Degree from [[Georgia Tech]] in 1984. During his tenure in the U.S. Air Force, Johnson was recognized as a Distinguished Graduate from the Air Force Reserve Officer Training Corps, Squadron Officers School, and Air Command & Staff College. Bill is also a recipient of the Air Force Meritorious Service Medal, the Air Force Commendation Medal, and the National Defense Service Medal. As Director of the Air Force’s Chief Information Officer Staff at U.S. Special Operations Command, Johnson worked directly with senior congressional and Secretary of Defense representatives, as well as top leaders within the various U.S. intelligence communities, to ensure America’s Special Operations forces were adequately equipped to carry out critical national security missions.<ref>{{cite web | title = About Bill Johnson | publisher = Bill Johnson for Congress Campaign Website | url = http://www.billjohnsonleads.com/about.html | accessdate = 2010-11-11| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20101111172421/http://www.billjohnsonleads.com/about.html| archivedate= 11 November 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref>

He co-founded Johnson-Schley Management Group, an [[information technology]] (IT) [[IT consulting|consulting company]] that increased revenues by more than 200% in just three years under his leadership. In 2003, he left the company to form J2 Business Solutions, where he provided executive level IT support as a [[defense contractor]] to the U.S. military. From 2006 to 2010, he served as [[Chief Information Officer]] of a global manufacturer of highly electronic components for the transportation industry.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2010#District 6*}}

In May 2010, Johnson defeated two primary opponents to earn the Republican nomination.<ref>{{Cite web| title =Wilson, Johnson win in 6th District| publisher = Marietta Times | date =2010-05-05| url =http://www.mariettatimes.com/page/content.detail/id/521619/Wilson--Johnson-win-in-6th-District.html?nav=5189| accessdate =2010-11-18 }}</ref> In the general election, Johnson won his bid by a 50.3-45.1%<ref name="PoliticoMargin">[http://www.politico.com/2010/maps/#/House/2010/OH The 2010 Results Maps - POLITICO.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> margin against incumbent [[Charlie Wilson (Ohio politician)|Charlie Wilson]]. He began his term in the [[112th United States Congress]] on January 3, 2011.

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 6}}
In November 2011, Wilson filed a rematch in the newly redrawn 6th District.<ref>http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/196335-former-rep-charlie-wilson-will-seek-rematch-in-ohio</ref> Johnson defeated Wilson once again in a heavily contested race, and began his second term in January of 2013.

===Tenure===
Bill Johnson is a member of both the Conservative [[Republican Study Committee]] and the centrist [[Republican Main Street Partnership]].  

During the 112th Congress, Johnson's "Veteran's Health Care Facilities Capital Improvement Act" was passed into law.<ref>{{cite web|title=Library of Congress- Thomas|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/D?d112:6:./temp/~bdIRSz::{{!}}/bss/|}}</ref>   Additionally, the House passed Johnson's "World War II Memorial Prayer Act" which would require the prayer President Franklin Roosevelt gave on D-Day to be placed on the World War II memorial.<ref>{{cite web|title=Library of Congress- Thomas|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/D?d112:2:./temp/~bdcbnv::{{!}}/bss/|}}</ref>  The House also passed Johnson's "Stop the War on Coal Act" which would stop the creation of any new rules that threaten mining jobs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Library of Congress- Thomas|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/D?d112:8:./temp/~bdTwmh:@@@D&summ2=m&{{!}}/bss/|}}</ref> Both pieces of legislation have been sent to the Senate for consideration.

Johnson is also the sponsor of H.R. 4036,  the "Pass a Budget Now Act" which would cut the pay of legislators if a budget is not passed by April 15 of each year.<ref>{{cite web|title=GovTrack.us|url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr4036}}</ref>   

;Abortion
In the 2010 Ohio Primary Election Candidate Survey, Johnson stated, "I am pro-life, and I oppose abortion except in the case of rape, incest, and when the mother's life is in danger. Additionally, I support parental notification and a ban on partial birth abortions." During his 2010 and 2012 general elections, Johnson received the endorsement of the Ohio Right to Life PAC.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ohio Right to Life|url=http://www.ohiovotesforlife.org/endorsements/}}</ref> 

;Gun Issues
A lifelong member of the [[National Rifle Association]], Johnson opposes restrictions on gun ownership.  He was endorsed by the NRA in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=NRA endorsements-Ohio|url=http://www.nrapvf.org/grades-endorsements/2012/ohio.aspx}}</ref> 

;Health care
Johnson opposes the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].{{fact|date=January 2013}}

;Gay Marriage
Johnson opposes the legalization of gay marriage and believes that it “undermines the integrity of the American family.” <ref>{{cite web | title = Bill Johnson: Issue Positions | publisher = Project Vote Smart | url = http://votesmart.org/candidate/political-courage-test/120649/bill-johnson/ | accessdate = 2011-11-20}}</ref>

===Legislation sponsored===
* [[Community Fire Safety Act of 2013 (H.R. 3588; 113th Congress)]] - Johnson introduced this bill into the House on November 21, 2013.<ref name=3588sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3588 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th/house-bill/3588|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=3 December 2013}}</ref> The bill would prevent the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] from requiring that all new [[fire hydrant]]s in the United States be [[lead]]-free beginning in 2014.<ref name=bothpartiesreject>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=Both parties reject EPA fire hydrant rule|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/votes/191828-gop-dems-reject-epa-rule-on-fire-hydrants|accessdate=3 December 2013|newspaper=The Hill|date=2 December 2013}}</ref> Johnson referred to the EPA's ruling as "absurd" and said that "it is unconscionable that the EPA has put our public safety at risk because during the hot summer months sometimes, somewhere kids may play in fire hydrant water."<ref name=epathreat1>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=House bill warns of EPA threat to fire hydrants|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/energyenvironment/191208-members-look-to-block-epa-rule-requiring-lead-free-fire|accessdate=3 December 2013|newspaper=The Hill|date=22 November 2013}}</ref>
* [[Preventing Government Waste and Protecting Coal Mining Jobs in America (H.R. 2824; 113th Congress)]] - Johnson introduced this bill into the House on July 25, 2013.<ref name=2824allactions>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2824 - All Actions|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/2824/all-actions/|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=4 March 2014}}</ref> If passed, the bill would amend the [[Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act of 1977]] to require state programs for regulation of [[Surface mining|surface coal mining]] to incorporate the necessary rule concerning excess spoil, coal mine waste, and buffers for perennial and intermittent streams published by the Office of Surface Mining Reclamation and Enforcement on December 12, 2008.<ref name=2824sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2824 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/2824|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=28 February 2014}}</ref> Supporters of the bill argue that it will be good for jobs, save the government money, and improve U.S. energy production by  preventing the Obama Administration from introducing more coal regulations.<ref name=SmartEnergy>{{cite web|title=Johnson, Lamborn Introduce Legislation To Protect Jobs, Help Stop Administration’s War on Coal|url=http://www.smartenergyuniverse.com/regulatory/19688-johnson-lamborn-introduce-legislation-to-protect-jobs-help-stop-administration-s-war-on-coal|publisher=Smart Energy Universe|accessdate=5 March 2014|date=3 March 2014}}</ref> Opponents of the bill described it as a bill that would require "OSM to implement the flawed 2008 Stream Buffer Zone rule and prevent the agency from improving that rule for a minimum of seven years."<ref name=EcoWatchBeans>{{cite news|last=Beans|first=Laura|title=House Republicans Use Fear Mongering In Fight for Mountaintop Removal Coal Mining|url=http://ecowatch.com/2013/08/08/republicans-fight-for-mountaintop-removal-coal-mining/|accessdate=5 March 2014|newspaper=EcoWatch|date=8 August 2013}}</ref>
* [[Improving Trauma Care Act of 2014 (H.R. 3548; 113th Congress)]] - Johnson introduced this bill into the House on November 20, 2013.<ref name=3548sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3548 - Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3548|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=24 June 2014}}</ref> It is a bill that would amend the [[Public Health Service Act]], with respect to trauma care and research programs, to include in the definition of "trauma" an [[injury]] resulting from extrinsic agents other than mechanical force, including those that are thermal, electrical, chemical, or radioactive.<ref name="3548sum"/>
