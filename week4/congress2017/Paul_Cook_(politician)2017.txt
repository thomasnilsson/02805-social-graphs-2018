'''Paul Joseph Cook''' (born March 3, 1943) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[U.S. House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 8th congressional district]] since 2013. Previously, he served as a member of [[California State Assembly]] from 2006 to 2012 and on the [[Yucca Valley]] city council from 1998 until 2006. He is a [[United States Republican Party|Republican]].

==Early life, education, and military career==
Cook was born in [[Meriden, Connecticut]] in 1943. He was raised in Meriden and did not permanently move to California until the end of his military career. In 1966, he graduated from [[Southern Connecticut State University]], earning a B.S. in teaching. Later that year, he joined the [[United States Marine Corps]]. As an infantry officer, Cook served in the [[Vietnam War]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cook.house.gov/about/full-biography|title=Biography of Col. Paul Cook (Ret.)|publisher=Cook.house.gov|accessdate=October 14, 2013}}</ref>

He later earned an [[Masters of Public Administration|MPA]] from [[California State University, San Bernardino]] in 1996 and a master's in political science from [[University of California Riverside]] in 2000. His actions in combat earned him many honors, including the [[Bronze Star]] and two [[Purple Heart]]s. He served in the Marine Corps for 26 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/paul-cook-r-calif-8.html|title=Paul Cook, R (Calif.-8th)|publisher=Roll Call|date=October 10, 2013|accessdate=October 14, 2013}}</ref>

==Academic career==
From 1993–1994, he was Director of Yucca Valley Chamber of Commerce. From 1998-2002, he was a professor at [[Copper Mountain College]]. Cook taught courses on political violence and terrorism at [[University of California Riverside]] since 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/california-8th-house-district-20121101 |title=California, 8th House District |publisher=NationalJournal.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

==California Assembly==

===Elections===
In 2006, Cook ran for California's 65th Assembly District. Cook won a five candidate Republican primary field with 29% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=217111 |title=CA State Assembly 65 – R Primary Race – Jun 06, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> In the general election, Cook defeated Democrat Rita Ramirez-Dean 60%–37%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=219294 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Democrat Carl Wood 53%–47%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=334722 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 04, 2008 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=York|first=Anthony|url=http://www.capitolweekly.net/article.php?_adctlid=vjq2q43wvsl855oxjrv6rjkdct1yk&issueId=xjptu9p7nq5i7a&xid=xjpuz5r83m9kbp|title=Legislative results include warning signs for Assembly Republicans|publisher=Capitol Weekly|accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> In 2010, he won re-election to a third term, defeating Wood again 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=656273 |title=CA State Assembly 65 Race – Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

===Tenure===
The 65th district included the cities of [[Banning, California|Banning]], [[Beaumont, California|Beaumont]], [[Big Bear Lake, California|Big Bear Lake]], [[Calimesa]], [[Cherry Valley, California|Cherry Valley]], [[Hemet]], [[Moreno Valley]], [[Perris]], [[San Jacinto, California|San Jacinto]], [[Sun City, Menifee, California|Sun City]], [[Twentynine Palms]], [[Yucaipa]], [[Yucca Valley]] and other smaller communities and unincorporated areas in [[Riverside County]] and [[San Bernardino County]].

The California Chamber of Commerce and the California Taxpayers Association gave Cook a perfect 100% rating from 2007-2011.<ref name="Joinpaulcook">{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.joinpaulcook.com/biography.asp|work=joinpaulcook.com|accessdate=3 January 2013}}</ref>  In 2010, Democratic [[Speaker of the California State Assembly|Speaker of the Assembly]] [[John Pérez]] appointed Cook to chair the Veterans Affairs Committee, the first time a Democratic speaker had appointed a Republican to chair a committee since 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://republican.assembly.ca.gov/inc/article.aspx?id=223004 |title=Assemblyman Cook Named Chair Of Assembly Veterans Affairs Committee |publisher=Republican.assembly.ca.gov |date=2010-03-19 |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 campaign===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 8}}
In January 2012, 34-year incumbent [[Jerry Lewis (California politician)|Jerry Lewis]] announced he would not seek re-election in November.  Cook entered the primary for the district, which had been renumbered from the 41st to the 8th in redistricting.  He finished second in the 13-candidate all-party open primary. He earned 15% of the vote. Fellow Republican and conservative activist Gregg Imus ranked first with 16% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=740812 |title=CA – District 08 – Open Primary Race – Jun 05, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref> Cook was endorsed by the California Off-Road Vehicle Association (CORVA) past presidents, the [[San Bernardino Sun]], National Vietnam and Gulf War Veterans Coalition, the County Farm Bureau, state Assemblyman [[Steve Knight (politician)|Steve Knight]], state Senator [[Sharon Runner]], and U.S. Congressman [[Ed Royce]].<ref name="Paul Cook">{{cite web|title=Join Paul Cook Blog {{!}} Thoughts from Colonel Paul Cook|url=http://www.joinpaulcook.com/blog/|publisher=Paul Cook|accessdate=1 March 2013}}</ref> In the November election, Cook defeated Imus 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=768683 |title=CA – District 08 Race – Nov 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-10-14}}</ref>
