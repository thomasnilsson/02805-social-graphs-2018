'''Juan C. Vargas''' (born March 7, 1961) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 51st congressional district]] since 2013. The district includes all of [[Imperial County, California|Imperial County]] as well as the southernmost portions of [[San Diego County, California|San Diego County]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wedrawthelines.ca.gov/downloads/meeting_handouts_082011/map_20110815_ap_cd_51_certified.pdf|title=District 51|date=August 2011|work=California Redistricting Commission certified map|publisher=Healthy City|accessdate=5 January 2013}}</ref>  He is a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]].

Vargas previously served in the [[California State Senate]] representing the 40th District, the [[California State Assembly]] representing the 79th district, and the [[San Diego City Council]].

==Early life and education==
{{BLP sources section|date=January 2013}}
Juan Vargas was born on an egg ranch in [[National City, California]], where he grew up very poor. He is the third of ten children of Tomas and Celina Vargas, who immigrated to the United States from [[Mexico]] in the late 1940s as part of the [[Bracero program]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vargas.house.gov/about/full-biography|title=Full Biography|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref> Vargas graduated [[Latin honors|Magna Cum Laude]] with a BA from [[University of San Diego]] and earned an MA in Humanities from [[Fordham University]] in [[New York City]].

After college, Vargas joined the [[Society of Jesus|Jesuit]] Novitiate (introduction) in [[Santa Barbara, California|Santa Barbara]]. In the Jesuits, Vargas served in an orphanage in the civil-war-torn jungles of El Salvador. After leaving the Jesuits, he decided on law school and graduated in 1991 with a [[Juris Doctor|JD]] from [[Harvard Law School]],<ref name = "senate" /> making him a classmate of President [[Barack Obama]].

==Early political career (1992-2000)==

===1992 congressional election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections, 1992}}
In 1992, he decided to run for the newly created [[California's 50th congressional district|50th Congressional District]], which was based in [[San Diego, California|San Diego]]. He lost the Democratic primary, ranking fourth with 19% of the vote. [[Bob Filner]] won the primary with a plurality of just 26%<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=207775|title=Our Campaigns - CA District 50 - D Primary Race - Jun 02, 1992|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref> and went on to win the election.

===San Diego City Council===
Vargas served on the [[San Diego City Council]] from 1993&ndash;2000. While on the Council, he created "Operation Restore" to employ [[Homelessness|homeless]] individuals to remove [[graffiti]] and to rehabilitate homes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.eastcountymagazine.org/node/11655|title=Meet Congressman Juan Vargas, who wins seat in 51st congressional district|last=Mork|first=Janis|date=November 7, 2012|work=East County Magazine|accessdate=9 November 2012}}</ref>

===1996 congressional election===
In 1996, Vargas decided to challenge Filner in the Democratic primary. Vargas wouldn’t debate Filner, so the incumbent instead sparred with a life-sized Vargas cardboard cutout. Vargas said he agrees with Filner on “99 percent” of issues.<ref>http://www.voiceofsandiego.org/government/article_4e6ac596-d848-11e1-91ca-001a4bcf887a.html</ref> Filner defeated him 55%-45%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=748257|title=Our Campaigns - CA District 50 - D Primary Race - Mar 26, 1996|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref>

==California State Assembly (2000-2007)==

===Elections===
In 2000, Vargas decided to run for [[California's 79th State Assembly district]]. He defeated Republican Jon Parungao 77%-19%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=210851|title=Our Campaigns - CA State Assembly 79 Race - Nov 07, 2000|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="ut1">{{cite web|url=http://legacy.signonsandiego.com/news/politics/20060607-0052-7n7duke.html|title=Bilbray edges out Busby|last=LaVelle|first=Philip J.|author2=Dani Dodge|date=June 7, 2006|publisher=San Diego Union-Tribune|accessdate=15 February 2011}}</ref> In 2002, he defeated Republican Mark Fast 66%-30%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5730|title=Our Campaigns - CA State Assembly 79 Race - Nov 05, 2002|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref> In 2004, he defeated Libertarian Eli Wallace Conroe 85%-15%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=72949|title=Our Campaigns - CA State Assembly 79 Race - Nov 02, 2004|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In his first year in the Assembly, he was appointed Assistant Majority Leader. He authored AB 188, which was legislation that bans smoking in children's playgrounds. He also introduced legislation aimed at protecting children from [[Arcade games|arcade video games]]. Vargas also authored legislation to mandate life sentences for people who commit violent sex crimes against children, which served as a model for Chelsea’s Law.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sd40.senate.ca.gov/biography|title=Biography|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref>

===2006 congressional election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2006#District 51}}
In 2006 Vargas decided to challenge Filner for a third time, this time in [[California's 51st congressional district]]. Vargas accused Filner of being a part of the [[Culture of corruption (2006)|culture of corruption]] of Washington, pointing out that Filner had paid his wife more than $500,000 in campaign funds for consulting services performed from their condominium in Washington. Filner in return argued that Vargas had controversial payments to his brother-in-law, who was a lobbyist for realtors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2010/person/bob-filner-ca/|title=Login|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.ivpressonline.com/2005-12-26/campaign-funds_24212116|title=Mudslinging, issues pit Vargas against Filner|work=Imperial Valley Press Online|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref> Filner defeated Vargas 51%-43%, with Danny Ramirez getting 6% of the vote.<ref name="ut1" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=212958|title=Our Campaigns - CA District 51 - D Primary Race - Jun 06, 2006|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rawstory.com/news/2006/Bigamy_bribery_other_allegations_plague_congressional_0605.html|title=Bigamy, bribery, other allegations plague California congressional race|last=Raftery|first=Miriam|date=June 5, 2006|publisher=The Raw Story|accessdate=15 February 2011}}</ref>

==Business career (2007-2009)==
After leaving the State Assembly in 2006 due to term limits, Vargas took a job with a home, auto and small business insurance company, where he was tasked with creating jobs and outreach in diverse San Diego Communities as part of the company's diversity initiative.{{citation needed|date=February 2011}} Vargas left that job at the end of 2009 to run as a Democratic candidate for the California State Senate.

==California State Senate (2010-2013)==

===2010 election===
In 2010 Vargas narrowly won a seat in the California State Senate, 40th District.  He defeated Assemblywoman [[Mary Salas]] by 22 votes, after recounts in San Diego and Riverside counties. He resigned from the Senate effective January 2, 2013 so that he could take his seat as a Congressman. A special election to fill his seat was held in March 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=Peters, Vargas ready for Washington fray|last=Walker|first=Mark|date=January 3, 2013|work=San Diego Union Tribune|accessdate=10 January 2013}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives (2012-Present)==

===2012 election===
In 2012 when Filner announced he would retire from Congress to run for [[Mayor of San Diego]], Vargas endorsed him despite their history of bitter rivalry.<ref>{{cite news |title=The Politics of Bob Filner’s Personality |author=Liam Dillon |url=http://www.voiceofsandiego.org/government/article_4e6ac596-d848-11e1-91ca-001a4bcf887a.html |newspaper=Voice of San Diego |date=2 November 2012 |accessdate=26 January 2013 |quote=Last summer, Juan Vargas walked up to Filner in a beer line at a South Bay festival with a message he’s never given Filner before. Vargas told Filner he was going to endorse him. }}</ref> Vargas then ran for Filner's seat in the [[California's 51st congressional district|51st district]]. In the open primary, he ranked first with 46% of the vote. Republican Michael Crimmins ranked second with 20%, Democratic State Senator [[Denise Moreno Ducheny]] came in third with 15%, and four other candidates received single digit percentages.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=735348|title=Our Campaigns - CA - District 51 - Open Primary Race - Jun 05, 2012|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref> In November, he defeated Crimmins 71%-29%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=768473|title=Our Campaigns - CA - District 51 Race - Nov 06, 2012|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-general/12-us-reps.pdf|title=Presidential General Election, Tuesday, November 5, 2012|work=United States Representative|publisher=California Secretary of State|accessdate=5 January 2013}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He and his wife Adrienne live in the [[Golden Hill, San Diego|Golden Hill]] area of San Diego and have two daughters. During the 1999 [[Kosovo War|armed conflict]] in Kosovo, Vargas welcomed a Kosovar refugee family into his family's home for nearly two years.<ref name = "senate">{{cite web|url=http://sd40.senate.ca.gov/biography|title=Senator Juan Vargas: California Senate District 40|work=California State Senate|accessdate=9 November 2012}}</ref>
