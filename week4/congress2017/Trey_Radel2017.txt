'''Henry Jude "Trey" Radel III''' (born April 20, 1976) is an author, actor, businessman, American politician and a former member of the [[United States House of Representatives]]. Radel represented [[Florida's 19th congressional district]] from January 3, 2013 through January 27, 2014.<ref name=trey-radel-resignation-102642>{{cite news|last1=Sherman|first1=Jake|last2=Isenstadt|first2=Alex|title=Radel resigns from House seat|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2014/01/trey-radel-resignation-102642|work=[[Politico]]|date=January 27, 2014|accessdate=January 27, 2014}}</ref>  The [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] had previously worked as a television reporter, [[news anchor|anchor]],  and conservative talk radio host. The district is located in southwest Florida and includes [[Fort Myers]], [[Naples, Florida|Naples]] and [[Cape Coral, Florida|Cape Coral]].

Radel is the author of Democrazy, which is described as "Primary Colors meets This Town: a brutally honest, outrageous, funny, and irreverent memoir by a former GOP congressman.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.amazon.com/Democrazy-Story-Weird-Politics-Finger/dp/0735210721/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1481923966&sr=8-1&keywords=trey+radel&utm_content=buffer6c547&utm_medium=social&utm_source=facebook.com&utm_campaign=buffer|title=Democrazy: A True Story of Weird Politics, Money, Madness, and Finger Food|last=Radel|first=Trey|date=2017-03-28|publisher=Blue Rider Press|isbn=9780735210721|language=English}}</ref>" Published by Blue Rider Press, an imprint of the [[Random House]] company, the book will be released March 28, 2017.

Radel also works as an actor, appearing in the show ''[[StartUp (TV series)|StartUp]]'' on the [[Sony]] owned App ''[[Crackle (company)|Crackle]]'' and most recently in ''Truth is Stranger than Florida'' on the [[Investigation Discovery]] network.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm7743975/|title=Trey Radel|website=IMDb|access-date=2016-12-18}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Trey Radel was born in 1976 in Cincinnati, the son of Kathleen (Sollinger) and Henry Jude Radel, Jr.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kathleen RADEL's Obituary on The Cincinnati Enquirer|url=http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/cincinnati/obituary.aspx?n=kathleen-radel&pid=148106949|website=[[Legacy.com]]|date=January 6, 2010}}</ref> He attended [[Elder High School]].<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.fox19.com/story/24015998/henry-trey-radel-charged-with-cocaine-possession | title=Fla. Congressman charged with cocaine possession has Cincinnati ties | publisher=WXIX | accessdate=November 19, 2013}}</ref> Radel majored in communications and minored in [[Italian language|Italian]] at [[Loyola University Chicago]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zoominfo.com/people/Radel_Trey_158879128.aspx |title=Trey Radel, Host, FOX News |publisher=ZoomInfo.com |accessdate= November 7, 2012}}</ref>

==Journalist career==
Radel was trained as an actor and a comedian and performed improvisational work at [[The Second City|Second City]] in Chicago.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://voice123.com/treyradel |title=Insert Name – A – Insert Name at Voice123 |publisher=Voice123.com |date=November 3, 2008 |accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref> Radel began his working career as a journalist, working as both an [[TV anchor|anchor]] and a reporter. He interned for [[CNN]] at its headquarters in [[Atlanta, Georgia|Atlanta]]. He then worked for the [[CBS]] affiliates in [[Houston, Texas|Houston]] and [[Chicago, Illinois|Chicago]]. He was a TV and radio talk show host of Daybreak, on [[WINK-TV]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://flnewscenter.com/?p=2053 |title=Florida News Center » Blog Archive » Radel takes job on conservative radio… |publisher= FlNewsCenter.com |date= |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> & [[WFSX-FM]] [[92.5 FM|92.5]] [[Fox News Radio]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.meetup.com/llc111/events/13629590/?action=detail&eventId=13629590 |title=Trey Radel from 92.5 Fox News Radio at SWFLYR meeting – Lee Liberty Caucus (Fort Myers, FL) |publisher=Meetup |date= |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>

He bought the ''Naples Journal'', a community newspaper in Naples, Florida, which he reformatted and expanded,<ref>{{cite web|title=Trey Radel for U.S. Congress|url=http://www.treyradel.com/|work= Treyradel.com|publisher=Friends of Trey Radel, Inc.|accessdate=November 18, 2012 |archiveurl= http://www.webcitation.org/6CHSK5vAj|archivedate=November 18, 2012}}</ref> later selling the paper to the ''[[Naples Daily News]]''.{{citation needed|date=November 2012}}  He founded Trey Communications [[Limited liability company|LLC]], a [[Conservativism in the United States|conservative]] media relations firm. The company purchased and sold [[domain name]]s, including some of a pornographic nature.<ref>{{cite web|last=Meneimer|first=Stephanie|title=Florida GOP Candidate Has History of Registering Sex-Themed Web Addresses|url=http://www.motherjones.com/politics/2012/06/florida-trey-radel-sex-domain-gate-scandal|publisher=Mother Jones|accessdate=November 8, 2012}}</ref>

He and his wife founded a [[nonprofit organization]] called the U.S. Forces Fund, which focuses on helping injured soldiers returning home from abroad.<ref>{{cite web|title=Florida Division of Corporations|url=http://www.sunbiz.org/COR/2012/0201/20194554.tif|accessdate=November 8, 2012}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
{{Main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2012#District 19}}
Republican 14th District Congressman [[Connie Mack IV]] decided not to run for reelection to his seat, in order to challenge [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] [[United States Senate|U.S. Senator]] [[Bill Nelson (politician)|Bill Nelson]]. Radel decided to run in the open seat, which had been renumbered as the 19th District.   Five other Republican candidates also filed to run. Controversy occurred when it was discovered that the campaign committee "Friends of Trey Radel, Inc." had purchased his opponents' domain names nearly a year before he announced he was going to run for office.<ref name="Domain Gate">{{cite web|last=Ward|first=Kenric|title=Trey Radel Shuts "Domain Gate" – Will Give Up Websites|url=http://www.sunshinestatenews.com/story/trey-radel-shuts-domain-gate-will-give-websites |publisher=Sunshine State News|accessdate=November 8, 2012}}</ref> When this was revealed, he denied knowing about it, blaming it on staff, but eventually admitted he had purchased the domain names.<ref name="Domain Gate"/> Subsequently, his campaign committee created websites and attached them to his opponents' domain names, purportedly for the purpose of disseminating the voting records of the opponents, which were posted on the websites.<ref name="Domain Gate" />  Radel's political philosophy is conservative, but he nevertheless has said he supports the principles of the [[DREAM Act]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.floridapoliticalpress.com/2012/07/29/trey-radel-just-cant-seem-to-avoid-controversy/ |title=» Trey Radel Just Can’t Seem To Avoid Controversy |publisher= FloridaPoliticalPress.com |date=July 29, 2012 |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> Radel was endorsed by the incumbent Connie Mack IV, former U.S. Senator [[Connie Mack III]], and former [[New York City Mayor]] [[Rudy Giuliani]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.treyradel.com/endorsements/ |title=Endorsements « Trey Radel |publisher=Treyradel.com |date= |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> Chauncey Goss (who finished second to Radel in the primary)<ref>{{cite web|title=Florida Division of Elections |url= http://doedoe.dos.state.fl.us/elections/resultsarchive/Index.asp?ElectionDate=8/14/2012&DATAMODE=}}</ref>  was endorsed by U.S. Congressman [[Paul Ryan]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Buzzacco |first=Jenna |url=http://www.naplesnews.com/news/2012/aug/14/trey-radel-southwest-florida-congressional-seat-19/ |title=Radel declares victory in Southwest Florida race for Congress |publisher= Naples Daily News |date= August 14, 2012 |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> Radel won the primary with 30% of the vote, primarily on the strength of his showing in his native Lee County.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=736120 |title=FL District 19 – R Primary Race – Aug 14, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns  |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>

In the general election Radel faced Democrat Jim Roach of Cape Coral, a retired [[General Motors|GM]] research engineer and Vietnam veteran.  However, Radel was heavily favored to win.  The 19th has long been reckoned as one of the most Republican districts in Florida, and as such the Republican primary was considered the real contest for the election.  In 2008, Republican presidential nominee [[John McCain]] won the district with 57% of the vote. In 2010, [[Rick Scott]] won the district with 61% of the vote.  Radel did indeed win the 2012 election, with 63% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tampabay.com/specials/2011/reports/redistricting/congress.shtml |title=Redistricting Florida U.S. House Districts &#124; Tampabay.com – St. Petersburg Times |publisher=Tampabay.com |date= |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> The ''Naples Daily News'' reported that the financial disclosure reports Radel submitted during his campaign were inaccurate and that once he was elected he amended these reports.<ref>{{cite web|last=Staats |first=Eric |url=http://www.naplesnews.com/news/2012/dec/17/radels-amended-financial-report-filed-day-after/ |title=Rep. Radel's amended financial report, filed day after election, shows much greater wealth » Naples Daily News |publisher=Naplesnews.com |date= |accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref> According to Radel's attorney he underreported his assets by failing to note his trust funds.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://doe.dos.state.fl.us/PublicRecordsBER/wfPublicImagesBER.aspx?account=55790 |title=Campaign Documents – Florida Department of State |publisher=Doe.dos.state.fl.us |date= |accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref>

Following his conviction for cocaine possession, Radel went on a self-imposed [[leave of absence]] following a  prompting the [[Republican Party of Florida]] and [[Governor of Florida]] [[Rick Scott]] to call for his resignation.<ref name="bbcresign" /> On January 27, 2014, Radel announced he would resign from Congress.<ref name="trey-radel-resignation-102642" /> He had not voted in Congress after November 15, 2013 in the wake of the conviction.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://firstread.nbcnews.com/_news/2014/01/02/22149207-out-of-rehab-radel-to-return-to-house-next-week?lite|title=Out of rehab, Radel to return to House next week|date=January 2, 2014|work=[[NBC News]]|last1=Thorp|first1=Frank}}</ref>

==Resignation==
On October 29, 2013, Radel was arrested in the District of Columbia after attempting to buy 3.5 grams of cocaine from an undercover federal officer.<ref name="bbcresign"/><ref name="guardianresign">{{Cite news | last = Luscombe | first = Richard | title = Florida congressman Trey Radel to resign after cocaine conviction | url = https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/jan/27/florida-congressman-trey-radel-resign-cocaine-conviction | publisher = The Guardian | date = January 27, 2014 | accessdate = January 27, 2014 }}</ref> Less than a month later, Radel pleaded guilty to a misdemeanor count of possession of cocaine and was sentenced to one year of supervised probation.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/11/trey-radel-cocaine-possession-pleads-guilty-100121.html  |title=Trey Radel pleads guilty to cocaine possession |publisher=''Politico'' |last=Sherman |first=Jake |date=November 20, 2013 |accessdate= November 20, 2013}}</ref>

In November 2013, Radel announced that he would be taking a leave of absence from Congress to undergo addiction rehabilitation and that he would be donating his salary to charity during his absence, but stopped short of resigning.<ref name="guardianresign"/><ref>{{cite web |last=Sherman|first=Jake|title=Trey Radel taking leave of absence from Congress |url= http://www.politico.com/story/2013/11/trey-radel-leave-of-absence-congress-cocaine-100183.html |publisher=Politico|accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref> Following his announcement, the [[Republican Party of Florida]] and [[Governor of Florida]] Rick Scott called on Radel to "resign immediately" and "focus his attention on rehabilitation and his family".<ref name="bbcresign"/><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sunshinestatenews.com/story/republicans-gear-replace-trey-radel-congress  |title=Republicans Gear Up to Replace Trey Radel in Congress |publisher=''Sunshine State News'' |last=Henderson |first=Jeff |date=November 28, 2013 |accessdate= November 29, 2013}}</ref> In late January 2014, Radel officially tendered his resignation in a letter to [[Speaker of the United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House]] [[John Boehner]]. Republican businessman [[Curt Clawson]] won the special general election held on June 24, 2014  to replace him.

Radel completed all conditions of his probation in October 2014, and his criminal record was wiped clean as a result.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Camia|first1=Catalina|title=Trey Radel's criminal record cleared of cocaine charge|url=http://onpolitics.usatoday.com/2014/10/31/trey-radel-cocaine-record-expunged/|website=USA Today Politics|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref> Spokesman for the U.S. attorney's office, William Miller said, "The court granted that request and the records are now expunged."<ref>{{cite web|last1=King|first1=Ledyard|title=Ex-congressman Trey Radel's record expunged|url=http://www.news-press.com/story/news/2014/10/30/ex-congressman-trey-radels-record-cleared/18218925/|website=News-press.com|accessdate=12 November 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Radel is married to [[WFTX-TV|FOX-4]] anchor Amy Wegmann.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://m.naplesnews.com/news/2012/jan/06/broadcaster-trey-radel-running-connie-macks-congre/ |title= Broadcaster Trey Radel running for Connie Mack’s congressional seat |publisher= Naples Daily News |date= January 6, 2012|accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>  They have one child, and live in Fort Myers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.naplesnews.com/news/2012/aug/05/congressional-candidates-who-running-lee-collier/ |title=Florida House District 19 race: Republican primary candidates |publisher= Naples Daily News |date= August 5, 2012 |accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>
