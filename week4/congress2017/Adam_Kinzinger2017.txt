'''Adam Daniel Kinzinger'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legistorm.com/memberbio/2749/Rep_Adam_Kinzinger_IL.html |title=Representative Adam Daniel Kinzinger (Adam) (R-Illinois, 16th) – Biography from |publisher=LegiStorm |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> (born February 27, 1978)<ref>{{cite news|title=112th Congress: Paul Gosar, Adam Kinzinger, R-Ill. (11th District) |first=Lauren |last=Smith |newspaper=[[Congressional Quarterly]] |date=November 6, 2010 |url=http://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101106/pl_cq_politics/politics000003759282 |accessdate=November 7, 2010 }}{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|IL|16}}. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. He was first elected to Congress in 2010, winning election to represent [[Illinois's 11th congressional district]]. After redistricting, he was re-elected to Congress in both 2012 and 2014 to represent [[Illinois's 16th congressional district]].

==Early life, education, and early political career==
Kinzinger was born in 1978 in [[Kankakee, Illinois|Kankakee]], the son of Betty Jo, an elementary school teacher, and Rus Kinzinger, a CEO of faith-based organizations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pantagraph.com/news/local/government-and-politics/elections/kinzinger-s-win-no-surprise-to-those-around-him/article_f31421e0-e7a5-11df-a415-001cc4c03286.html|title=Kinzinger's win no surprise to those around him|first=Michele|last=Steinbacher|work=pantagraph.com|accessdate=5 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2011-06-21/opinion/ct-oped-0622-freshmen-20110616_1_adam-kinzinger-bobby-schilling-bob-dold|title=How's it Going? - A Q&A with Illinois' 5 freshman congressmen|date=June 21, 2011|work=chicagotribune.com|accessdate=5 August 2016}}</ref> He was raised in [[Bloomington, Illinois]]. He graduated from [[Normal Community West High School]] in 1996 and earned a bachelor's degree from [[Illinois State University]] in 2000.

In 1998, while a student at Illinois State, Kinzinger ran for election as a County Board member in [[McLean County, Illinois]]. He won, defeating an incumbent County Board member. Kinzinger remained on the board until 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stoptheaclu.com/archives/2009/03/03/adam-kinzinger-for-illinois-11th-hero-patriot/|title=Adam Kinzinger For Illinois 11th — Hero, Patriot|date=2009-03-03|publisher=Stop The ACLU|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090306174056/http://www.stoptheaclu.com/archives/2009/03/03/adam-kinzinger-for-illinois-11th-hero-patriot|archivedate=2009-03-06|accessdate=2015-06-23}}</ref>

Kinzinger worked as an intern for former U.S. Senator [[Peter Fitzgerald (senator)|Peter Fitzgerald]] shortly after his graduation from Illinois State, as part of a program offered there.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cas.illinoisstate.edu/pol/about/kinzingeraward.shtml |title=Department Alumn Congressman Adam Kinzinger Awarded Outstanding Young Alumni Award |publisher=Illinois State University |accessdate=May 11, 2015 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518080524/http://cas.illinoisstate.edu/pol/about/kinzingeraward.shtml |archivedate=May 18, 2015 |df= }}</ref>

In 2003, Kinzinger joined the [[United States Air Force]]. He was commissioned a [[2nd Lieutenant]] in November 2003 and later awarded his [[Aircrew Badge|pilot wings]]. Kinzinger was initially a [[Boeing KC-135 Stratotanker|KC-135 Stratotanker]] pilot and flew missions in [[South America]], [[Guam]], [[Iraq]] and [[Afghanistan]]. He later switched to flying the [[RC-26B Metroliner|RC-26]] [[surveillance aircraft]] and was stationed in Iraq twice.<ref>{{cite web|author=Chuck Sweeny |url=http://www.rrstar.com/insight/x2079019554/Chuck-Sweeny-Manzullo-challenger-Kinzinger-got-politics-bug-early |title=Chuck Sweeny: GOP's Adam Kinzinger got politics bug early – News – Rockford Register Star – Rockford, IL |publisher=Rrstar.com |date=2012-01-10 |accessdate=2015-03-14 }}{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Kinzinger has served in the [[Air Force Special Operations Command]], [[Air Combat Command]], [[Air Mobility Command]], and [[Air National Guard]] and was progressively promoted to his current rank of [[Major]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://electadam.com/inner.asp?z=1|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090630233256/http://www.electadam.com/inner.asp?z=1|archivedate=2009-06-30|accessdate=2015-06-23|title=Biography|publisher=Adam Kinzinger for U.S.Congress}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections ===

====2010====
{{main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2010#District 11*}}
In January 2009, Kinzinger met Republican U.S. Congressmen [[Mike Pence]], [[Mark Kirk]], and [[Peter Roskam]] to discuss a possible run for Congress.<ref name=ak>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/issues/54_74/atr/31446-1.html |title=Illinois: First GOPer Lines Up to Take On Halvorson : Roll Call Politics |publisher=Rollcall.com |date=2009-01-20 |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> Kinzinger decided to run in [[Illinois' 11th congressional district]], held by [[Democratic Party (U.S.)|Democratic]] U.S. Congresswoman [[Debbie Halvorson]]. He started campaigning full-time in May 2009, when he returned home from his 3rd tour in Iraq. In the early autumn, Kinzinger held eight town halls across the 11th congressional district that were attended by nearly 2,000 people. He was endorsed by former [[Alaska]] Governor [[Sarah Palin]]. On February 2, 2010, Kinzinger won the five-candidate Republican primary with 64% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=512637 |title=IL District 11-R Primary Race – Feb 02, 2010 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

In the general election, he was endorsed by the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' and the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]''. On November 2, 2010, Kinzinger defeated Halvorson 57%–43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=489035 |title=IL – District 11 Race – Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

====2012====
{{main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2012#District 16}}
After redistricting, Kinzinger's district was eliminated, and a large slice of its territory was merged with the [[Rockford, Illinois|Rockford]]-based [[Illinois' 16th congressional district|16th District]], represented by fellow Republican [[Don Manzullo]], a 67-year-old politician first elected in 1992.  Kinzinger had represented 31% of the district, while Manzullo had represented at least 44% of the district, prior to redistricting. In the March Republican primary, Kinzinger defeated Manzullo, 56%–44%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=734301 |title=IL – District 16 – R Primary Race – Mar 20, 2012 |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref> In the general election, Kinzinger defeated Democrat Wanda Rohl, 62%–38%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/IL |title=2014 Election Results Senate: Map by State, Live Midterm Voting Updates |publisher=Politico.com |date= |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

====2014====
{{main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2014#District 16}}
In 2014, Kinzinger was targeted by the [[Club for Growth]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/285193-club-for-growth-targeting-rino-republicans|title=Club for Growth targeting 9 'RINO' Republicans for primary challenges – The Hill's Ballot Box|last=Jaffe|first=Alexandra|date=February 27, 2013|publisher=Thehill.com|accessdate=March 2, 2015}}</ref> In the Republican primary, he faced David Hale, a nurse and founder of the [[Rockford, Illinois|Rockford]] [[Tea Party movement|Tea Party]]. Kinzinger won with 78% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sweeny|first=Chuck|date=September 12, 2013|title=Chuck Sweeny: Tea Party's David Hale to challenge Adam Kinzinger|url=http://www.rrstar.com/x1367240960/Chuck-Sweeny-Tea-Partys-Hale-to-challenge-Kinzinger|newspaper=[[Rockford Register Star]]|location=Rockford, Illinois|accessdate=March 2, 2015}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://www.elections.il.gov/Downloads/ElectionInformation/VoteTotals/2014GPOfficialVote.pdf|title=Official Illinois State Board of Elections Results|accessdate=March 2, 2015}}</ref>

In the general election, Kinzinger faced Democratic nominee Randall Olsen; he won with 71% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|last=Misener|first=Jacob|date=December 5, 2013|title=Democratic challenger emerges in 16th District race|url=http://www.pontiacdailyleader.com/article/20131205/NEWS/131209661/1001/NEWS|newspaper=[[The Daily Leader]]|location=Pontiac, Illinois|accessdate=March 2, 2015}}</ref><ref name="Generalelection">{{cite web |url=http://www.elections.il.gov/ElectionInformation/GetVoteTotals.aspx |title=Illinois General Election 2014|publisher=Illinois State Board of Elections|date=2014-11-04 |accessdate=2015-03-02}}</ref>

==== 2016 ====
{{main article|United States House of Representatives elections in Illinois, 2016#District 16}}
Kinzinger won the March 2016 Republican primary with 100% of the vote.<ref name=primaryresults>{{cite web|url=https://www.elections.il.gov/ElectionResults.aspx?ID=50 |title=2016 Illinois primary results, March 15, 2016 |publisher=Chicago Tribune |accessdate=May 13, 2016}}</ref> No candidates filed for the Democratic primary for his seat.

On August 3, 2016, Kinzinger announced publicly that he would not support GOP presidential nominee [[Donald Trump]]. "I'm an American before I'm a Republican," he told CNN's [[Wolf Blitzer]], adding that "I'm a Republican because I believe that Republicanism is the best way to defend the United States of America… [Trump] throws all of these Republican principles on their head." Kinzinger noted, however, that he also would not support Democratic Party presidential nominee [[Hillary Clinton]], and was mulling other options.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cnn.com/2016/08/03/politics/election-2016-adam-kinzinger-american-before-a-republican/index.html |title=GOP congressman says he can't support Trump: 'I'm an American before I'm a Republican'|publisher=CNN|date=2016-08-03 |accessdate=2016-08-03}}</ref>

===Tenure===

In 2010 Kinzinger signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any [[global warming]] legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://americansforprosperity.org/noclimatetax//wp-content/uploads/2010/01/kinzinger.pdf |format=PDF |title=Americans for Prosperity Applauds U.S. House Candidate Adam Kinzinger |publisher=Americansforprosperity.org |accessdate=2015-03-14}}</ref>

Kinzinger sponsored the [[Veteran Emergency Medical Technician Support Act of 2013]], H.R. 235 in the [[113th United States Congress|113th Congress]].<ref name=235overview>{{cite web|title=H.R. 235 – Congress.gov|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/235|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=April 1, 2013}}</ref> This legislation would make it easier for veterans with emergency medical technician training in the military to get civilian licenses to perform the same job outside of the military. The bill passed the House of Representatives by a [[voice vote]] on February 12, 2013 and was referred to the [[United States Senate]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Bill Summary & Status – H.R. 235 – All Congressional Actions|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d113:H.R.235:@@@X|publisher=Library of Congress|accessdate=April 1, 2013}}</ref>

On June 5, 2014, Kinzinger introduced a [[To require the Secretary of Energy to prepare a report on the impact of thermal insulation on both energy and water use for potable hot water|bill (H.R. 4801; 113th Congress)]] which would require the [[United States Secretary of Energy]] to prepare a report on the effects that [[thermal insulation]] has on both energy consumption and systems for providing [[potable water]] in [[:Category:Buildings of the United States government|federal buildings]].<ref name=cbo4801>{{cite web|title=CBO – H.R. 4801|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/45466|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=23 June 2014}}</ref><ref name=ASEcommitteepass>{{cite web|last1=LaFreniere|first1=Kelsey|title=Alliance Vice-Chair Rep. Kinzinger Pushes For Energy Efficiency|url=https://www.ase.org/blog/alliance-vice-chair-rep-kinzinger-pushes-energy-efficiency|publisher=Alliance to Save Energy|accessdate=23 June 2014|date=11 June 2014}}</ref> Kinzinger argued that "with the federal government being the single largest consumer of energy in the country, doing our best to maximize the potential savings from improved insulation systems is a commonsense step I think everybody can agree on."<ref name="ASEcommitteepass"/>

Kinzinger is a member of both the [[Republican Study Committee]] and the more moderate [[Republican Main Street Partnership]].{{citation needed|date=October 2015}}

Conservative Review graded Kinzinger's 2016 voting record as an "F", with a Liberty Score of 35%.  Out of 247 Republicans in the House of Representatives, 37 received a lower grade (had a more liberal voting record).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conservativereview.com/scorecard|title=Liberty Scorecard - Conservative Review|work=conservativereview.com|accessdate=5 August 2016}}</ref> The [[American Conservative Union]] (ACU) awarded Kinzinger a somewhat higher score, giving him a Lifetime Rating of 59.60 out of 100.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://acuratings.conservative.org/acu-federal-legislative-ratings/?year1=2015&chamber=12&state1=26&sortable=1 |title=Federal Legislative Ratings|publisher=American Conservative Union|accessdate=2016-08-03}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2006, the Wisconsin Red Cross named Kinzinger "Hero of the Year" for wrestling a knife-wielding man to the ground and disarming him. The man had cut the throat of a woman on a street in downtown [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]].<ref>[http://www.pantagraph.com/articles/2006/08/18/news/doc44e4dec184663319987097.txt ]{{dead link|date=March 2015}}</ref> Recalling the event in an interview, Kinzinger said "The whole time it was, to me, kind of a done deal that I was going to get stabbed in the process, but I knew that this wasn't something I could wake up to ... everyday with that memory that I watched her die."<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4TvCyQasadw|title=Adam Kinzinger saves woman's life/Milwaukee TV report|publisher=YouTube|date=2010-06-22|accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref> The woman survived. For this act Kinzinger also received the United States Air Force [[Airman's Medal]] and the National Guard's Valley Forge Cross for Heroism.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://illinoisreview.typepad.com/illinoisreview/2009/01/kinzinger-consider-challenging-halvorson-in-11th-cd.html|accessdate=2015-06-03|title=Kinzinger considers challenging Halvorson in 11th CD|publisher=Illinois Review|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091212151205/http://illinoisreview.typepad.com/illinoisreview/2009/01/kinzinger-consider-challenging-halvorson-in-11th-cd.html|archivedate=2009-12-12|date=2009-01-16}}</ref>

In 2011, Kinzinger was ranked 5th on ''[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]'s'' annual "50 Most Beautiful People" list, which ranks anyone who regularly works on Capitol Hill.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/173451-50-most-beautiful-people-2011-washington-congress-capitol?page=0%2C4|title=50 Most Beautiful People for 2011|publisher=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]|date=2011-07-27|accessdate=2015-06-23|archivedate=2015-06-23|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150623144652/http://thehill.com/capital-living/173451-50-most-beautiful-people-2011-washington-congress-capitol?page=0%2C4}}</ref>

In 2011, Kinzinger became engaged to a fellow pilot, Air Force Captain Riki Meyers; the engagement was called off in 2012.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Goodin|first1=Emily|title=Rep. Kinzinger's wedding called off|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/in-the-know/in-the-know/272741-rep-kinzingers-wedding-delayed|accessdate=27 October 2015|publisher=The Hill|date=December 13, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Skiba|first1=Katherine|title=Rep. Adam Kinzinger of Illinois gets engaged|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2011-12-21/news/ct-talk-adam-kinzinger-engaged-1221-20111221_1_republican-congressman-don-manzullo-face-veteran-fellow-pilot|accessdate=27 October 2015|publisher=Chicago Tribune|date=December 21, 2011}}</ref>
