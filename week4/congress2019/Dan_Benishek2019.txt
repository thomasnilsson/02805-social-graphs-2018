'''Daniel Joseph "Dan" Benishek''' (born April 20, 1952) is an [[Americans|American]] [[physician]] and politician who was the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|MI|1}} from 2011 to 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.city-data.com/elec2/elec-CRYSTAL-FALLS-MI.html |title=CRYSTAL-FALLS, Michigan (MI) Political Contributions by Individuals – Donations to Congress, local, or national races, Republican or Democrat and other candidates |publisher=City-data.com |date= |accessdate=2013-11-11}}</ref> He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

==Early life, education, and medical career==

Benishek was born in [[Iron River, Michigan]] in 1952,<ref name=CQ>{{cite news|title=112th Congress: Dan Benishek, R-Mich. (1st District) |first=John |last=Reichard |newspaper=[[Congressional Quarterly]] |date=November 3, 2010 |url=https://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101103/pl_cq_politics/politics000003758269 |accessdate=November 7, 2010 }}{{dead link|date=December 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> the son of Helen (née Kovaleski) and Joseph Benishek. Three of his paternal great-grandparents were Bohemian (Czech) immigrants, while his maternal grandparents were [[Polish people|Polish]] [[Immigration to the United States|immigrants]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/benishek.htm |title=Dan Benishek ancestry |publisher=[[Rootsweb]] |date= |accessdate=2013-11-11}}</ref> His father was killed in local iron mines in 1957, so Benishek was raised by his widowed mother and extended family. He worked in the family business, the Iron River Hotel, until he went to college. He graduated from the [[University of Michigan]] in 1974 with a Bachelor of Science in Zoology.<ref>[http://govrel.umich.edu/congress.html], UM Government relations.</ref> From there, he graduated from [[Wayne State University School of Medicine]] in 1978. Prior to his election to Congress, he was a [[general surgeon]] in the [[Dickinson County Healthcare System]].<ref name="imdn-3-2010"/>

==U.S. House of Representatives elections==

===2010 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2010#District 1}}

Benishek formally announced his candidacy for Congress on March 16, 2010, running against incumbent [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Bart Stupak]].<ref name="imdn-3-2010">{{cite news| url = http://www.ironmountaindailynews.com/page/content.detail/id/514588.html | title = Benishek runs for Congress | work = Iron Mountain Daily News | first = Lisa | last = Hoffman | date = March 16, 2010 | accessdate = March 31, 2011}}</ref> Stupak's vote in support of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]] fueled an outpouring of support for Benishek, who had no Internet presence aside from a basic website on the day the bill was passed. He received more than $50,000 in unsolicited donations in the first 48 hours after Stupak's vote for the bill on March 21, 2010.<ref>{{cite news | publisher=[[National Public Radio]] | accessdate= March 30, 2010 | url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=125259922 | title=Yoopers Reserved Over Stupak's 'Yes' Vote | first = David | last = Schaper | date = March 28, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | work = [[Wall Street Journal]] | url = https://blogs.wsj.com/washwire/2010/03/23/stupak-challenger-gains-support/ | title = Stupak Challenger Gains Support | last = Farnam | first = T.W. | date = March 23, 2010 | accessdate = March 31, 2011}}</ref> "It's amazing, I just can't believe it," he said regarding the support he has gotten. "I need to use this momentum to repeal this health care bill and I'm going to do it.  We're going to take over, the Republicans are going to regain the House of Representatives and we're going to repeal this health care bill."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uppermichiganssource.com/templates/2015_Page_Error?list=~%5Cnews%5Clists%5Clocal&id=435526|title=WLUC - Upper Michigan News, Weather, and Sports - WLUC - Home|first=|last=WLUC|publisher=|accessdate=15 June 2017}}</ref> ''[[The American Spectator]]'' called him "The Most Popular Republican in America" on March 21, in the wake of Stupak's yea vote.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://spectator.org/blog/2010/03/21/dan-benishek-just-became-the-m |title=Dan Benishek Just Became the Most Popular Republican in America |publisher=''[[The American Spectator]]'' |date=March 21, 2010 |accessdate=March 30, 2010 |author=[[Robert Stacy McCain]] |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100324212542/http://spectator.org/blog/2010/03/21/dan-benishek-just-became-the-m |archivedate=March 24, 2010 |df= }}</ref> Stupak announced his retirement on April 9, 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/04/10/us/politics/10stupak.html|title=Under Fire for Abortion Deal, Stupak to Retire|publisher=''[[The New York Times]]''|date=2010-04-09 | first=Monica | last=Davey | accessdate=April 26, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://politicalticker.blogs.cnn.com/2010/04/09/stupak-to-announce-retirement/?fbid=p-ueJJgDRIL | work=CNN | title=Stupak to announce retirement | accessdate=April 26, 2010 | date=April 9, 2010}}</ref> Benishek won the Republican primary against State Senator [[Jason Allen (Michigan politician)|Jason Allen]] by 15 votes.<ref>[http://www.michigan.gov//documents/sos/Official_Numbers_330332_7.pdf Election results] Michigan Government</ref>

On November 2, 2010, in the general election, Dan Benishek defeated Democratic nominee State Representative [[Gary McDowell]], Independent Glenn Wilson, [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] Keith Shelton, [[Green Party (United States)|Green]] Ellis Boal, and [[Constitution Party (United States)|UST]] Patrick Lambert. Benishek was sworn into office on January 5, 2011, as a member of the [[112th United States Congress]]; he succeeded Bart Stupak.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://elections.nytimes.com/2010/results/michigan | work=The New York Times | title=Michigan Election Results}}</ref>

===2012 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2012#District 1}}

Benishek faced re-election against McDowell in the 2012 election cycle. A September 20, 2012 [[Public Policy Polling]] poll showed the race as a statistical dead heat, with McDowell leading Benishek 44% to 42%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Michigan 1st Congressional District Survey Results|url=http://www.realclearpolitics.com/docs/2012/PPP_MI1_0920.pdf|publisher=Real Clear Politics|accessdate=31 July 2013}}</ref> Benishek was re-elected by a margin of 2,297 votes, less than 1% of the total votes cast.<ref>{{cite news|last=McGillivary |first=Brian |title=Benishek won by less than 1 percent |url=http://record-eagle.com/2012election/x2082765102/Benishek-won-by-less-than-1-percent |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130731205333/http://record-eagle.com/2012election/x2082765102/Benishek-won-by-less-than-1-percent |dead-url=yes |archive-date=31 July 2013 |accessdate=31 July 2013 |newspaper=Traverse City Record Eagle |date=7 November 2012 }}</ref>
Benishek endorsed [[Herman Cain]] in the 2012 Republican presidential primary.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1111/67675.html |title=Herman Cain lies low, snags endorsement |author=Tim Mak |date=5 November 2011 |work=Politico |publisher= |accessdate=7 November 2011}}</ref>

===2014 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2014#District 1}}
Benishek's opponent for the 2014 midterm elections was Democratic nominee [[Jerry Cannon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlive.com/politics/index.ssf/2013/08/retired_general_jerry_cannon_t.html|title=Retired general, Gitmo commander Jerry Cannon to challenge Dan Benishek for U.S. House seat in 2014|publisher=|accessdate=15 June 2017}}</ref> In October 2014, the ''Rothenberg Political Report'' moved the district from one that "tilts Republican" to one that is now "Republican favored."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Currier|first1=Craig|title=Benishek doubles Cannon's campaign bankroll|url=http://www.petoskeynews.com/featured-pnr/benishek-doubles-cannon-s-campaign-bankroll/article_cc5f2900-688b-535d-be4c-b4135ddefd50.html|accessdate=20 October 2014|publisher=Petoskey News|date=20 October 2014}}</ref> Benishek won re-election with 52% of the vote.

===2016 election===
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2016#District 1}}
After temporarily considering running for a fourth term, Benishek decided not to seek re-election in 2016; he endorsed Republican State Sen. [[Tom Casperson]] as his successor.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Spangler|first1=Todd|title=U.S. Rep. Benishek endorses Casperson for seat|url=http://www.freep.com/story/news/local/michigan/2016/01/14/us-rep-benishek-endorses-casperson-seat/78782272/|accessdate=15 April 2016|work=Detroit Free Press|date=January 14, 2016}}</ref>

===Legislation sponsored===
On January 4, 2013, Benishek introduced {{USBill|113|HR|163}}, a bill identical to the [[Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore Conservation and Recreation Act (S. 23; 113th Congress)]], which was introduced into the U.S. Senate by [[Carl Levin]].<ref name=163related>{{cite web|title=H.R. 163 – Related Bills|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/163/related-bills|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=4 March 2014}}</ref> The bill would designate as wilderness about 32,500 acres of the [[Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore]] in the state of Michigan. The newly designated lands and inland waterways would comprise the Sleeping Bear Dunes Wilderness, a new component of the [[National Wilderness Preservation System]].<ref name=cbos23>{{cite web|title=CBO – S. 23|url=http://cbo.gov/publication/44025|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=3 March 2014}}</ref> Benishek expressed his pleasure that [[Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore Conservation and Recreation Act|the bill]] "was developed locally," describing it as "the ideal way federal land management should occur, with input from the local communities."<ref name=Thompson1>{{cite news|last=Thompson|first=Carol|title=Sleeping Bear Dunes legislation moves forward|url=http://www.record-eagle.com/local/x1196465244/Sleeping-Bear-Dunes-legislation-moves-forward|accessdate=4 March 2014|newspaper=Traverse City Record-Eagle|date=1 March 2014}}</ref> The Senate version, S. 23, passed the Senate and was signed into law by President Obama on March 13, 2014.

On May 21, 2013, Benishek introduced the [[Demanding Accountability for Veterans Act of 2013 (H.R. 2072; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would require the Inspector General (IG) of the [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs]] (VA) to take additional action if the VA has not appropriately responded to an IG report that recommends actions to be taken by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to address a VA public health or safety issue.<ref name=2072crs>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2072 – Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/2072|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=27 May 2014}}</ref> The Secretary would be required to act swiftly on such IG reports, with the bill specifying actions to take.<ref name="2072crs"/> The Secretary would also be forbidden from giving any bonuses to managers with unresolved issues.<ref name="2072crs"/><ref name="GovExecKatz"/> Benishek said that the bill targets "bureaucrats in Washington who drag their feet and don't do their jobs."<ref name=GovExecKatz>{{cite news|last=Katz|first=Eric|title=Prohibiting VA Senior Exec Bonuses for 5 Years Would Save $18 Million|url=http://www.govexec.com/pay-benefits/2013/08/prohibiting-va-senior-exec-bonuses-5-years-would-save-18-million/69011/|accessdate=27 May 2014|newspaper=Government Executive|date=20 August 2013}}</ref>

===Political positions===
Benishek favors reduced government spending and a smaller government, and is [[pro-life]] and pro-gun rights.<ref>[http://www.thecypresstimes.com/article/News/National_News/CONTRIBUTIONS_NOW_POURING_IN_FOR_STUPAKS_OPPONENT/28650 CONTRIBUTIONS NOW POURING IN FOR STUPAK’S OPPONENT]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100325191307/http://www.thecypresstimes.com/article/News/National_News/CONTRIBUTIONS_NOW_POURING_IN_FOR_STUPAKS_OPPONENT/28650 |date=March 25, 2010 }}</ref> In 2014, he received an A+ grade from the [[National Rifle Association#NRA Political Victory Fund|National Rifle Association Political Victory Fund]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=VanDyke|first1=Todd|title=The Keweenaw Report|url=http://www.keweenawreport.com/news/benishek-receives-nra-endorsement/|accessdate=15 April 2016|date=September 26, 2014}}</ref> He supports [[Term limits in the United States|term limits]] and said before he was elected in 2010, "I am happy to tell voters I strongly favor term limits. Three terms and you’re retired seems about right to me." |url=http://blogs.rollcall.com/rothenblog/is-dan-benishek-waffling-on-term-limits/ |title=Is Dan Benishek Waffling on Term Limits? |author= Stu Rothenberg |publisher=Roll Call |accessdate=11 September Free Press|date=March 24, 2015}}</ref> In September 2015, he reverted course, announcing that he would in fact retire at the end of his current term rather than seeing re-election in 2016.<ref>{{cite
