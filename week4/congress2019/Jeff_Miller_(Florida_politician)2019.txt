'''Jefferson Bingham Miller''' (born June 27, 1959) is an American politician who served as the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|Florida|1|}}, from 2001 to 2017. He is a member of the [[United States Republican Party|Republican Party]]. The district includes all of [[Escambia County, Florida|Escambia]], [[Santa Rosa County, Florida|Santa Rosa]], [[Okaloosa County, Florida|Okaloosa]], [[Walton County, Florida|Walton]], [[Holmes County, Florida|Holmes]], and [[Washington County, Florida|Washington]] Counties.

==Early life, education, and career==
Miller was born in [[St. Petersburg, Florida]]. After graduating from Bronson High School in 1977, he served for one year under the [[National FFA Organization]] as Florida state Secretary. Miller went on to receive a Bachelor of Arts in Journalism<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nndb.com/people/061/000039941/|title=Jeff Miller at NNDB}}</ref> from the [[University of Florida]] in 1984.<ref name="FL Rep">{{cite web|url=http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/Sections/Representatives/details.aspx?MemberId=4152&SessionId=51|title=Jeff Miller's Florida House of Representatives Profile}}</ref> He was a [[real estate broker]] and a [[deputy sheriff]] before taking public office.<ref name="Profile">{{cite web|url=http://jeffmiller.house.gov/index.cfm?FuseAction=Bio.Home|title=Jeff Miller's Profile|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060301165947/http://jeffmiller.house.gov/index.cfm?FuseAction=Bio.Home|archivedate=2006-03-01|df=}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Miller lives in the small town of [[Chumuckla, Florida]] (Native American word for "Healing Waters"), which is located about 20&nbsp;miles northeast of [[Pensacola, Florida]]. He and his wife Vicki have two children and four grandchildren and are members of Olive Baptist Church.

==Florida legislature==
Miller was an executive assistant to Democratic state [[Florida Commissioner of Agriculture|Agriculture Commissioner]] [[Doyle Conner]] from 1984 to 1988. He was a Democrat until he switched parties in 1997.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/external/pre-election/bios/1240.html|title=Jeff Miller's AP Candidate Profile}}</ref>

Miller was a member of the [[Florida House of Representatives]] from 1998 to 2001.<ref name="FL Rep"/> During his three years there, he was a member of the committees on [http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/Sections/Committees/committeesdetail.aspx?CommitteeId=1919&SessionId=51 Utilities & Telecommunications], [http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/Sections/Committees/committeesdetail.aspx?CommitteeId=2004&SessionId=51 Congressional Redistricting], [http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/Sections/Committees/committeesdetail.aspx?CommitteeId=1915&SessionId=51 Council for Ready Infrastructure], and [http://www.myfloridahouse.gov/Sections/Committees/committeesdetail.aspx?CommitteeId=2001&SessionId=51 Rules, Ethics & Elections]. During his second term in the state house, Miller was chairman of the Utilities of Telecommunications Committee. He also oversaw a board in charge of community development in Escambia County.

After Republican incumbent [[Joe Scarborough]] resigned five months into his fourth term, Miller won a crowded six-way special Republican primary with 54 percent of the vote. He won the special election with 66 percent of the vote.  He won a full term in 2002 with 75 percent of the vote, and was reelected six more times with only nominal opposition.

Miller announced that he would not seek reelection during the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2016|2016 elections]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Isern|first=Will|url=http://www.pnj.com/story/news/2016/03/10/jeff-miller-not-seek-election/81585348| title=Jeff Miller will not seek re-election|date=March 10, 2016|accessdate=March 11, 2016|work=[[Pensacola News Journal]]}}</ref>
