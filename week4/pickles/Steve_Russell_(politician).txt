'''Steven Dane Russell''' (born May 25, 1963) is a retired American soldier and politician. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he is the member-elect of the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|OK|5}}.<ref>Casteel, Chris. "[http://newsok.com/republican-steve-russell-elected-to-represent-oklahoma-city-in-congress/article/5363552 Republican Steve Russell elected to represent Oklahoma City in Congress]," 'The Oklahoman' November 4, 2014.</ref> He is a former member of the [[Oklahoma Senate]]. 

==Early life==
Russell was born in [[Oklahoma City, Oklahoma]] to Clyde E. "Gene" Russell and Donna J. Porter Russell on May 25, 1963. Russell grew up in [[Del City, Oklahoma]]. The youngest of three, Russell nearly died at birth due to an opposite blood type with his mother. After weeks of intensive care, he recovered and would face several other brushes with death. At the age of seven, Russell was nearly killed by the deadly tornado that hit near Lake Eufala, Oklahoma on June 11, 1970 while visiting his grandparents. Later that year, Russell nearly died from a ruptured appendix and underwent two surgeries with weeks of intensive care at Midwest City Hospital.

Russell attended school at Del City Elementary and Kerr Junior High. He became a Christian reading a [[Gideons International|Gideon's]] [[New Testament]] he picked up at Kerr in 1977. Graduating from Del City High in 1981, Russell was President of his senior class and voted most likely to succeed.<ref>Aquila, Vol. 29, Del City High School, 1981/</ref> After earning a four-year [[Army ROTC]] scholarship, Russell attended [[Ouachita Baptist University]] in [[Arkadelphia, Arkansas]]. After graduating with a degree in Public Speaking, Russell was commissioned a [[Second Lieutenant]] in the [[U.S. Army]] [[Infantry]].

==Military career==
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:Lt. Col. Steve Russell, near Tikrit, Iraq, December, 2003.jpg|thumb|upright|Lt. Col. Steve Russell near Tikrit Iraq, December 2003]] -->
Receiving his Infantry training at [[Fort Benning]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], Russell married Cindy Myers, whom he met at Ouachita, on December 21, 1985. Russell would graduate from the U.S. Army Infantry School Officers Basic Course, Airborne School, Mortar School and a year later from the rigorous [[U.S. Army Ranger School]] in Class 11–87.<ref>http://www.benning.army.mil/infantry/rtb/Graduates/images/11-87.jpg.  Russell is standing 3rd row down from the 'G'/</ref>

Russell served 21 years in Airborne, Light and Mechanized assignments in the [[Arctic]], the desert, [[the Pacific]], in [[Europe]] and in the [[Continental United States]]. He served more than 7 years overseas and deployed operationally to [[Kosovo]], [[Kuwait]], [[Afghanistan]] and [[Iraq]].

During [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]], Russell commanded the 1st Battalion, [[22nd Infantry Regiment (United States)|22nd Infantry]] 'Regulars' and conducted combat in [[Tikrit, Iraq]] from the spring of 2003 to the spring of 2004. His task force was a part of Colonel [[James Hickey (soldier)|James Hickey]]'s 1st Brigade, 4th Infantry Division. Russell's battalion was broadly covered during the first year of the war by [[CNN]], [[Fox News Channel]], [[ABC News|ABC]], [[NBC News|NBC]], [[CBS News|CBS]], ''[[Time (magazine)|TIME]]'', [[Associated Press]] and [[Reuters]]. His unit was a central player in the hunt and capture of [[Saddam Hussein]] and has been featured in the [[Discovery Channel]]'s ''Ace in the Hole''<ref>Ace in the Hole {{cite web|url=https://video.search.yahoo.com/video/play?p=Ace+in+the+Hole+Documentary&vid=637d55195372e13b036f0aadf4111b32&l=1%3A29%3A30&turl=http%3A%2F%2Fts1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%3Fid%3DV.4837624790649732%26pid%3D15.1&rurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fwatch%3Fv%3DSznP8bD-I3E&tit=Ace+in+the+Hole+-+Capturing+Saddam+Hussein.mp4&c=0&sigr=11aeq80fv&sigt=11el856c5&ct=p&age=0&&tt=b}}</ref> and BBC Panorama's 'Saddam on the Run' documentaries.

Russell is highly decorated,<ref name="infantry1">{{cite web|url=http://1-22infantry.org/current/russell.htm |title=1st Battalion 22nd Infantry Former CO elected to senate |publisher=1-22infantry.org |date=August 27, 2008 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> having received the [[Legion of Merit]], the [[Bronze Star]] Medal with Valor Device<ref>{{cite web|author=iPad iPhone Android TIME TV Populist The Page |url=http://content.time.com/time/video/player/0,32068,4769005001_1866333,00.html |title=Revisiting a Tikrit Ambush - Video - TIME.com |publisher=Content.time.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> and Oak Leaf Cluster, the [[Combat Infantryman's Badge]] and his unit in Iraq was awarded the [[Valorous Unit Award]] for its role in the hunt and capture of Saddam Hussein.<ref>Valorous Unit Award, April 15, 2003 to Mar 15, 2004, 1st Battalion 22nd Infantry cited; Permanent Orders 031-14, 2008, Streamer embroidered IRAQ 2003–2004</ref>

Turning down a [[Queen's University]] Fellowship to [[Kingston, Canada]] for the [[U.S. Army War College]] and promotion to [[Colonel]], Russell retired as a [[Lieutenant Colonel]] in 2006. He returned to [[Oklahoma]] with his wife and five children.

==Post-military career==
Russell began an active veterans advocacy career upon retirement and worked closely with other veterans to bolster the soldiers voice in the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan. He formed Vets for Victory with several decorated warriors holding rallies in several states to embolden the public to support the troops.<ref>{{cite web|author=Brian C. Scherr, 2006 |url=http://vets4victory.com/rally_okla.html |title=rally_okla |publisher=Vets4victory.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> Russell also toured the country with many distinguished and highly decorated veterans such as [[David Bellavia]], author of ''[[House to House]]''; [[Marcus Luttrell]], author of ''[[Lone Survivor (book)|Lone Survivor]]''; Jeremiah Workman, author of ''Shadow of the Sword''; and Sean Parnell, author of ''[[Outlaw Platoon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vetsforfreedom.org/heroestour/|title=Vets for Freedom|work=vetsforfreedom.org|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.defendfreedomtour.com/vets-on-board/ |title=Vets on Board |publisher=Defend Freedom Tour |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>

As a motivational speaker, Russell embarked on a successful speaking career in hundreds of cities since 2007. He has been featured as a [[keynote]] speaker at such notable events as the Ring Banquet at the [[U.S. Air Force Academy]] and was honored as the [[Paul R. Smith]] Award recipient for his distinguished service in Iraq at the American Veterans Center 15th Annual Awards Gala in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.americanveteranscenter.org/2013/01/2012-awards-gala-broadcast/ |title=2012 Awards Gala Broadcast |publisher=American Veterans Center |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He is managed by [[Premiere Speakers Bureau]] of [[Franklin, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Steve Russell |url=http://premierespeakers.com/steve_russell |title=Steve Russell &#124; Premiere Motivational Speakers Bureau |publisher=Premierespeakers.com |date=July 25, 2012 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>

Russell has also been a successful writer and author. His opinion pieces have appeared in many major publications, and he is the author of ''We Got Him! A Memoir of the Hunt and Capture of Saddam Hussein'', published by [[Simon and Schuster]] in December 2011. An earlier version of the work was published by Deeds Publishing that year. The book has received critical acclaim for its vivid portrayal of combat and the events surrounding the hunt for the Iraqi dictator and his capture.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nyjournalofbooks.com/book-review/we-got-him-memoir-hunt-and-capture-saddam-hussein |title=a book review: We Got Him! A Memoir of the Hunt and Capture of Saddam Hussein |publisher=Nyjournalofbooks.com |date=December 6, 2011 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> It received numerous endorsements to include General [[Tommy Franks]] and the forward was written by General [[Ray Odierno]].

Russell also has appeared on many national television and radio shows as a military and foreign affairs commentator. In addition, he has made appearances on Spike TV's ''[[Deadliest Warrior]]'' and four episodes of [[Outdoor Channel]]'s ''[[Gun Stories]]''.

Russell is also the owner and founder of Two Rivers Arms, a small rifle manufacturing business that makes copies of the Iraqi Tabuk AK-47.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tworiversarms.com |title=index.html |publisher=Tworiversarms.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news9.com/category/116601/video-page?clipId=8210573&autostart=true |title=Videos – News9.com – Oklahoma City, OK – News, Weather, Video and Sports &#124; |publisher=News9.com |date=February 4, 2014 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news9.com/story/20929806/del-city-gun-shop-working?autoStart=true&topVideoCatNo=default&clipId=8299842 |title=Del City Gun Shop Struggling To Keep Up With Demand – News9.com – Oklahoma City, OK – News, Weather, Video and Sports &#124; |publisher=News9.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>

==Political career==
Russell began his service to the public in 2008 when he defeated four opponents in one of the most contested races in the [[Oklahoma State Senate]] that year. Russell won the initial Republican primary with 42% of the vote, leading by 15 points. He won the Republican run off with 67% of the vote.<ref name="infantry1"/>

As a State Senator Russell authored the Soldiers Relief Act that made Oklahoma’s serving military tax exempt, affecting over 50,000 soldiers and their families.<ref>{{cite web|author=Julie Bisbee &bull; |url=http://newsok.com/editor-comments-on-military-tax-break/article/3846413 |title=Editor comments on military tax break |publisher=News OK |date=June 16, 2009 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He fought for the rights of the unborn, against [[human trafficking]], and authored landmark adoption reform (three of Russell's five children are adopted).<ref>{{cite web|last=Mcnutt |first=Michael |url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-legislative-adoption-task-force-could-get-more-time/article/3362558 |title=Oklahoma Legislative adoption task force could get more time |publisher=News OK |date=April 18, 2009 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He also authored the Soldiers Credentialing Act, the law that allows credit for soldiers skills through direct certification rather than lengthy schooling allowing them to quickly enter the work force. He was also the lawmaker most known for the defense of the 2nd Amendment, restoring lost firearms freedoms and as a staunch defender of the Bill of Rights. Shortly after the publication of his book, Russell announced he would not seek reelection and he decided to focus on his business, public speaking and veterans advocacy.<ref>{{cite web|last=Associated |first=The |url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-state-senator-wont-seek-another-term/article/3612973 |title=Oklahoma state senator won't seek another term |publisher=News OK |date=October 13, 2011 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>

In January 2014, Russell announced he would seek the seat of the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|OK|5}}, which is being vacated by [[James Lankford]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://kfor.com/2014/01/21/former-state-sen-steve-russell-announces-plans-to-run-for-lankfords-congressional-seat/ |title=Former State Sen. Steve Russell announces plans to run for Lankford’s congressional seat |publisher=KFOR.com |date=January 21, 2014 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.steverussellforcongress.com|title=Steve Russell for Congress|work=steverussellforcongress.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On June 24, 2014, Russell led the field in the Republican Primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-elections-steve-russell-and-patrice-douglas-head-to-republican-runoff-in-5th-congressional-district-race/article/4952799|title=Oklahoma elections: Steve Russell and Patrice Douglas head to Republican runoff in 5th Congressional District race|work=NewsOK.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On Tuesday, August 26th, 2014, Russell won a major victory despite being heavily outspent to win the Republican nomination.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-elections-russell-easily-wins-gop-nod-in-5th-district-congressional-race-will-face-mcaffrey-in-general-election/article/5336158|title=Oklahoma elections: Russell easily wins GOP nod in 5th District congressional race, will face McAffrey in general election|work=NewsOK.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On November 4th, 2014, Russell was elected to Congress with 60.1% of the vote, handily defeating Democratic State Senator Al McCaffrey and three Independent candidates, Buddy Ray, Tom Boggs and Robert Murphy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ok.gov/elections/support/ok_results_seb.html#USREP|title=State Election Results, General Election, November 4, 2014|work=ok.gov|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Russell has been married 29 years to the former Cindy Myers of [[Columbus, Arkansas]]. He has five grown children, three of which are adopted from [[Hungary]]. He is a fifth generation Oklahoman.

==Education==
Russell is a graduate of Del City High, Class of 1981. He was inducted into the Del City Hall of Fame in its very first class along with professional football player [[Bob Kalsu]] who was killed in Vietnam and two-time Olympic Gold Medalist [[John Smith (wrestler)|John Smith]].

Russell graduated from [[Ouachita Baptist University]] with a BA in Public Speaking. He was the president and pledge master of his fraternity and cadet corps commander of the [[Army Reserve Officers' Training Corps|ROTC Cadets]].

Russell also earned a MMAS in History from the [[Command and General Staff College]] at [[Fort Leavenworth]], [[Kansas]] in 1998. His other military education includes the Infantry Officers Basic Course, the Armored Officers Career Course, the Combined Arms Staff School and the Battalion Commanders Course.

Russell's military skills schools include, Airborne School, Ranger School, Northern Warfare Summer and Winter Phase, Mortar School, and the Bradley Gunners and Commanders Courses.

Russell began his service to the public in 2008 when he defeated four opponents in one of the most contested races in the [[Oklahoma State Senate]] that year. Russell won the initial Republican primary with 42% of the vote, leading by 15 points. He won the Republican run off with 67% of the vote.<ref name="infantry1"/>

As a State Senator Russell authored the Soldiers Relief Act that made Oklahoma’s serving military tax exempt, affecting over 50,000 soldiers and their families.<ref>{{cite web|author=Julie Bisbee &bull; |url=http://newsok.com/editor-comments-on-military-tax-break/article/3846413 |title=Editor comments on military tax break |publisher=News OK |date=June 16, 2009 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He fought for the rights of the unborn, against [[human trafficking]], and authored landmark adoption reform (three of Russell's five children are adopted).<ref>{{cite web|last=Mcnutt |first=Michael |url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-legislative-adoption-task-force-could-get-more-time/article/3362558 |title=Oklahoma Legislative adoption task force could get more time |publisher=News OK |date=April 18, 2009 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He also authored the Soldiers Credentialing Act, the law that allows credit for soldiers skills through direct certification rather than lengthy schooling allowing them to quickly enter the work force. He was also the lawmaker most known for the defense of the 2nd Amendment, restoring lost firearms freedoms and as a staunch defender of the Bill of Rights. Shortly after the publication of his book, Russell announced he would not seek reelection and he decided to focus on his business, public speaking and veterans advocacy.<ref>{{cite web|last=Associated |first=The |url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-state-senator-wont-seek-another-term/article/3612973 |title=Oklahoma state senator won't seek another term |publisher=News OK |date=October 13, 2011 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>

In January 2014, Russell announced he would seek the seat of the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|OK|5}}, which is being vacated by [[James Lankford]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://kfor.com/2014/01/21/former-state-sen-steve-russell-announces-plans-to-run-for-lankfords-congressional-seat/ |title=Former State Sen. Steve Russell announces plans to run for Lankford’s congressional seat |publisher=KFOR.com |date=January 21, 2014 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.steverussellforcongress.com|title=Steve Russell for Congress|work=steverussellforcongress.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On June 24, 2014, Russell led the field in the Republican Primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-elections-steve-russell-and-patrice-douglas-head-to-republican-runoff-in-5th-congressional-district-race/article/4952799|title=Oklahoma elections: Steve Russell and Patrice Douglas head to Republican runoff in 5th Congressional District race|work=NewsOK.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On Tuesday, August 26th, 2014, Russell won a major victory despite being heavily outspent to win the Republican nomination.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-elections-russell-easily-wins-gop-nod-in-5th-district-congressional-race-will-face-mcaffrey-in-general-election/article/5336158|title=Oklahoma elections: Russell easily wins GOP nod in 5th District congressional race, will face McAffrey in general election|work=NewsOK.com|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref> On November 4th, 2014, Russell was elected to Congress with 60.1% of the vote, handily defeating Democratic State Senator Al McCaffrey and three Independent candidates, Buddy Ray, Tom Boggs and Robert Murphy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ok.gov/elections/support/ok_results_seb.html#USREP|title=State Election Results, General Election, November 4, 2014|work=ok.gov|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Russell has been married 29 years to the former Cindy Myers of [[Columbus, Arkansas]]. He has five grown children, three of which are adopted from [[Hungary]]. He is a fifth generation Oklahoman.

==Education==
Russell is a graduate of Del City High, Class of 1981. He was inducted into the Del City Hall of Fame in its very first class along with professional football player [[Bob Kalsu]] who was killed in Vietnam and two-time Olympic Gold Medalist [[John Smith (wrestler)|John Smith]].

Russell graduated from [[Ouachita Baptist University]] with a BA in Public Speaking. He was the president and pledge master of his fraternity and cadet corps commander of the [[Army Reserve Officers' Training Corps|ROTC Cadets]].

Russell also earned a MMAS in History from the [[Command and General Staff College]] at [[Fort Leavenworth]], [[Kansas]] in 1998. His other military education includes the Infantry Officers Basic Course, the Armored Officers Career Course, the Combined Arms Staff School and the Battalion Commanders Course.

Russell's military skills schools include, Airborne School, Ranger School, Northern Warfare Summer and Winter Phase, Mortar School, and the Bradley Gunners and Commanders Courses.
