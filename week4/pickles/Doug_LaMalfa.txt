'''Douglas L. LaMalfa''' (born July 2, 1960) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]]. A [[California Republican Party|Republican]], he has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 1st congressional district]], located in Northern California, since 2013. LaMalfa previously served in the [[California State Assembly]], representing the [[California's 2nd State Assembly district|2nd district]], from 2002 to 2008, and he served in the [[California State Senate]], representing the [[California's 4th State Senate district|4th district]], from 2010 to 2012.

==Early life, education, and early career==
LaMalfa is a fourth generation rice farmer and a lifelong Northern California resident. He and his wife, Jill, along with their four children, make their home on the family farm in [[Richvale, California]]. LaMalfa graduated from [[California Polytechnic State University|Cal Poly San Luis Obispo]] with a [[Bachelor's degree]] in Ag/Business. He manages the family farming business originally founded by his great-grandfather in 1931.

He was elected as a founding director of the California Rice Commission from Butte County. LaMalfa also served on the Domestic Promotion Committee of the USA Rice Federation. He was chairman of the Richvale Foundation Boosters, an organization dedicated to maintaining the profitability of the Richvale Café, a community meeting facility. LaMalfa is an owner and manager of the Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership. Since 1995, Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership has received payments totaling $5,132,156 in federal commodity subsidies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://farm.ewg.org/persondetail.php?custnumber=009247988 |title=Subsidy Summary &#124;&#124; EWG Farm Subsidy Database |publisher=Farm.ewg.org |date=2004-11-29 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

==California Assembly==

===Elections===
In 2002, LaMalfa ran for the [[California Assembly]] in the 2nd District. He won the Republican primary with 59% of the vote,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=286835 |title=CA State Assembly 02- R Primary Race - Mar 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> and the general election with 67%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5606 |title=CA State Assembly 02 Race - Nov 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> He won re-election in 2004 (68%)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71853 |title=CA State Assembly 02 Race - Nov 02, 2004 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> and 2006 (68%).<ref>http://vote.ss.ca.gov/Returns/stasm/0259.htm</ref>

===Tenure===
LaMafla worked with [[Bernie Richter]] as an early supporter of [[California Proposition 209 (1996)|Proposition 209]], which ended [[affirmative action]] in California. He also worked for passage of the Protection of Marriage Act, [[California Proposition 22 (2000)|Proposition 22]], which banned [[Same-sex marriage]] in California, and after that initiative was overturned by the courts, he was an early supporter and active in the [[California Proposition 8 (2008)|Proposition 8]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.douglamalfa.com/about/ |title=About Doug LaMalfa &#124; Doug LaMalfa for U.S. Representative |publisher=Douglamalfa.com |date=2013-11-21 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> LaMalfa authored ACA 22 to protect private property owners from government abuse of eminent domain. He also wrote the Forest Fire Protection Act of 2004 to allow landowners to make their rural lands fire-safe and without forest management.

LaMalfa opposed [[Mike Feuer]]'s [[microstamping]] bill, AB 1471, which was signed into law by Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] on October 13, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|author=Feuer |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/07-08/bill/asm/ab_1451-1500/ab_1471_bill_20071013_chaptered.html |title=AB 1471 Assembly Bill - CHAPTERED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> He opposed Speaker [[Fabian Núñez]]'s bill AB 8, which was designed to mandate businesses of a certain size to provide health insurance to their employees.

LaMalfa was a co-author of ACA 20, which, similar to Arizona's new law, would empower law enforcement to act as Immigration, Customs Enforcement Agents and would have cracked down on illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|author=Haynes |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/05-06/bill/asm/ab_0001-0050/aca_20_bill_20050628_amended_asm.html |title=ACA 20 Assembly Constitutional Amendment - AMENDED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> ACA 20 failed to pass the first hearing by a 3-to-6 vote.

In 2007, he successfully passed AB 1645, a law that would prevent seizures of firearms in the event of an emergency or natural disaster.  This was the first pro-gun legislation passed and signed into law in a decade. LaMalfa was named the California Rifle and Pistol Associations "Legislator of the Year". {{Citation needed|date=November 2012}}

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012}}

In January 2012, U.S. Congressman [[Wally Herger]] announced that he was retiring and said "Senator LaMalfa is running for Congress, and will do so with the endorsement of Congressman Herger."<ref>{{cite web|last=Sabalow |first=Ryan |url=http://www.redding.com/news/2012/jan/09/herger-announce-retirement-lamalfa-run-his-seat/ |title=UPDATED: Herger announces retirement; backs LaMalfa to run for his seat » Redding Record Searchlight |publisher=Redding.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> LaMalfa won the primary election in June 2012 with 38% of the vote in an eight-person race and winning 10 out of the district's 11 counties.<ref>http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-primary/pdf/13-sov-summary.pdf</ref> On November 6, 2012, LaMalfa defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] candidate Jim Reed 57%–43%.<ref>http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-general/sov-complete.pdf</ref>

===2014 election===

In the 2014 Primary Election, LaMalfa faced criticism for accepting millions of dollars in farm subsidies, while supporting legislation to drastically cut food stamps. LaMalfa was challenged in the 2014 Congressional Election by Democrat Heidi Hall however Hall Lost reciveing less than 35% of the Vote.

====Tenure====
In 2013 LaMalfa signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any Global Warming legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>http://americansforprosperity.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/LaMalfa_Doug.pdf</ref>

'''Douglas L. LaMalfa''' (born July 2, 1960) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]]. A [[California Republican Party|Republican]], he has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 1st congressional district]], located in Northern California, since 2013. LaMalfa previously served in the [[California State Assembly]], representing the [[California's 2nd State Assembly district|2nd district]], from 2002 to 2008, and he served in the [[California State Senate]], representing the [[California's 4th State Senate district|4th district]], from 2010 to 2012.

==Early life, education, and early career==
LaMalfa is a fourth generation rice farmer and a lifelong Northern California resident. He and his wife, Jill, along with their four children, make their home on the family farm in [[Richvale, California]]. LaMalfa graduated from [[California Polytechnic State University|Cal Poly San Luis Obispo]] with a [[Bachelor's degree]] in Ag/Business. He manages the family farming business originally founded by his great-grandfather in 1931.

He was elected as a founding director of the California Rice Commission from Butte County. LaMalfa also served on the Domestic Promotion Committee of the USA Rice Federation. He was chairman of the Richvale Foundation Boosters, an organization dedicated to maintaining the profitability of the Richvale Café, a community meeting facility. LaMalfa is an owner and manager of the Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership. Since 1995, Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership has received payments totaling $5,132,156 in federal commodity subsidies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://farm.ewg.org/persondetail.php?custnumber=009247988 |title=Subsidy Summary &#124;&#124; EWG Farm Subsidy Database |publisher=Farm.ewg.org |date=2004-11-29 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

'''Douglas L. LaMalfa''' (born July 2, 1960) is an [[United States|American]] [[politician]]. A [[California Republican Party|Republican]], he has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[California's 1st congressional district]], located in Northern California, since 2013. LaMalfa previously served in the [[California State Assembly]], representing the [[California's 2nd State Assembly district|2nd district]], from 2002 to 2008, and he served in the [[California State Senate]], representing the [[California's 4th State Senate district|4th district]], from 2010 to 2012.

==Early life, education, and early career==
LaMalfa is a fourth generation rice farmer and a lifelong Northern California resident. He and his wife, Jill, along with their four children, make their home on the family farm in [[Richvale, California]]. LaMalfa graduated from [[California Polytechnic State University|Cal Poly San Luis Obispo]] with a [[Bachelor's degree]] in Ag/Business. He manages the family farming business originally founded by his great-grandfather in 1931.

He was elected as a founding director of the California Rice Commission from Butte County. LaMalfa also served on the Domestic Promotion Committee of the USA Rice Federation. He was chairman of the Richvale Foundation Boosters, an organization dedicated to maintaining the profitability of the Richvale Café, a community meeting facility. LaMalfa is an owner and manager of the Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership. Since 1995, Dsl Lamalfa Family Partnership has received payments totaling $5,132,156 in federal commodity subsidies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://farm.ewg.org/persondetail.php?custnumber=009247988 |title=Subsidy Summary &#124;&#124; EWG Farm Subsidy Database |publisher=Farm.ewg.org |date=2004-11-29 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

==California Assembly==

===Elections===
In 2002, LaMalfa ran for the [[California Assembly]] in the 2nd District. He won the Republican primary with 59% of the vote,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=286835 |title=CA State Assembly 02- R Primary Race - Mar 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> and the general election with 67%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5606 |title=CA State Assembly 02 Race - Nov 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> He won re-election in 2004 (68%)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71853 |title=CA State Assembly 02 Race - Nov 02, 2004 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> and 2006 (68%).<ref>http://vote.ss.ca.gov/Returns/stasm/0259.htm</ref>

===Tenure===
LaMafla worked with [[Bernie Richter]] as an early supporter of [[California Proposition 209 (1996)|Proposition 209]], which ended [[affirmative action]] in California. He also worked for passage of the Protection of Marriage Act, [[California Proposition 22 (2000)|Proposition 22]], which banned [[Same-sex marriage]] in California, and after that initiative was overturned by the courts, he was an early supporter and active in the [[California Proposition 8 (2008)|Proposition 8]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.douglamalfa.com/about/ |title=About Doug LaMalfa &#124; Doug LaMalfa for U.S. Representative |publisher=Douglamalfa.com |date=2013-11-21 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> LaMalfa authored ACA 22 to protect private property owners from government abuse of eminent domain. He also wrote the Forest Fire Protection Act of 2004 to allow landowners to make their rural lands fire-safe and without forest management.

LaMalfa opposed [[Mike Feuer]]'s [[microstamping]] bill, AB 1471, which was signed into law by Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] on October 13, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|author=Feuer |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/07-08/bill/asm/ab_1451-1500/ab_1471_bill_20071013_chaptered.html |title=AB 1471 Assembly Bill - CHAPTERED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> He opposed Speaker [[Fabian Núñez]]'s bill AB 8, which was designed to mandate businesses of a certain size to provide health insurance to their employees.

LaMalfa was a co-author of ACA 20, which, similar to Arizona's new law, would empower law enforcement to act as Immigration, Customs Enforcement Agents and would have cracked down on illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|author=Haynes |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/05-06/bill/asm/ab_0001-0050/aca_20_bill_20050628_amended_asm.html |title=ACA 20 Assembly Constitutional Amendment - AMENDED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> ACA 20 failed to pass the first hearing by a 3-to-6 vote.

In 2007, he successfully passed AB 1645, a law that would prevent seizures of firearms in the event of an emergency or natural disaster.  This was the first pro-gun legislation passed and signed into law in a decade. LaMalfa was named the California Rifle and Pistol Associations "Legislator of the Year". {{Citation needed|date=November 2012}}

He also worked for passage of the Protection of Marriage Act, [[California Proposition 22 (2000)|Proposition 22]], which banned [[Same-sex marriage]] in California, and after that initiative was overturned by the courts, he was an early supporter and active in the [[California Proposition 8 (2008)|Proposition 8]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.douglamalfa.com/about/ |title=About Doug LaMalfa &#124; Doug LaMalfa for U.S. Representative |publisher=Douglamalfa.com |date=2013-11-21 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> LaMalfa authored ACA 22 to protect private property owners from government abuse of eminent domain. He also wrote the Forest Fire Protection Act of 2004 to allow landowners to make their rural lands fire-safe and without forest management.

LaMalfa opposed [[Mike Feuer]]'s [[microstamping]] bill, AB 1471, which was signed into law by Governor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] on October 13, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|author=Feuer |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/07-08/bill/asm/ab_1451-1500/ab_1471_bill_20071013_chaptered.html |title=AB 1471 Assembly Bill - CHAPTERED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> He opposed Speaker [[Fabian Núñez]]'s bill AB 8, which was designed to mandate businesses of a certain size to provide health insurance to their employees.

LaMalfa was a co-author of ACA 20, which, similar to Arizona's new law, would empower law enforcement to act as Immigration, Customs Enforcement Agents and would have cracked down on illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|author=Haynes |url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/05-06/bill/asm/ab_0001-0050/aca_20_bill_20050628_amended_asm.html |title=ACA 20 Assembly Constitutional Amendment - AMENDED |publisher=Leginfo.ca.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> ACA 20 failed to pass the first hearing by a 3-to-6 vote.

In 2007, he successfully passed AB 1645, a law that would prevent seizures of firearms in the event of an emergency or natural disaster.  This was the first pro-gun legislation passed and signed into law in a decade. LaMalfa was named the California Rifle and Pistol Associations "Legislator of the Year". {{Citation needed|date=November 2012}}

==California Senate==

===2010 election===
In 2010, he ran for the [[California State Senate]] in the 4th District. In the Republican primary, he defeated State Representative [[Rick Keene]] 58%–42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=653299 |title=CA State Senate 04- R Primary Race - Jun 08, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Lathe Gill 68%–32%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=653297 |title=CA State Senate 04 Race - Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In November 2011, LaMalfa led the Republican opposition against a proposed [[high-speed rail|bullet train]] saying "In light of the High Speed Rail plan that was submitted and that the numbers still do not work, California in this dire fiscal crisis that we're in, we're going to introduce legislation to repeal the HSR Authority and the funding for that the state was going to put forward."<ref>[http://www.kmjnow.com/pages/landing?Move-Afoot-to-Derail-CAs-Bullet-Train-Pr=1&blockID=562442&feedID=806 ]{{dead link|date=April 2014}}</ref>

LaMalfa gave Democrat [[Governor of California|Governor]] [[Jerry Brown]] a "C" for his fiscal policies in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newstalk1290.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/gov-brown-releases-may-revise-to-close-state-deficit/ |title=Gov. Brown Releases May Revise to Close State Deficit &#124; Newstalk1290 KPAY |publisher=Newstalk1290.wordpress.com |date=2011-05-17 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

LaMalfa opposed [[FAIR Education Act|a bill]] that would require history teachers in all [[Education in California|California public schools]] to teach [[History of homosexuality|history of homosexuality and gay civil rights]]. LaMalfa stated the Governor Brown was "out of touch with what I think are still mainstream American values. That's not the kind of stuff I want my kids learning about in public school. They've really crossed a line into a new frontier."<ref>[http://www.sacbee.com/2011/07/15/3771352/gov-brown-signs-law-requiring.html#storylink=cpy ]{{dead link|date=April 2014}}</ref>

LaMalfa strongly opposed the [[National Popular Vote Interstate Compact]], which would eliminate the [[Electoral College (United States)|Electoral College]]. He argued "I think this is dangerous. It flies in the face of 220 years of election law. We have an electoral college; it was put there for a reason."<ref>{{cite web|author=DON THOMPSON, Associated Press1:10 p.m.July 14, 2011 |url=http://www.utsandiego.com/news/2011/jul/14/calif-lawmakers-approve-change-to-electoral-votes/ |title=Calif lawmakers approve change to electoral votes |publisher=UTSanDiego.com |date=2011-07-14 |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===2012 election===
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012}}

In January 2012, U.S. Congressman [[Wally Herger]] announced that he was retiring and said "Senator LaMalfa is running for Congress, and will do so with the endorsement of Congressman Herger."<ref>{{cite web|last=Sabalow |first=Ryan |url=http://www.redding.com/news/2012/jan/09/herger-announce-retirement-lamalfa-run-his-seat/ |title=UPDATED: Herger announces retirement; backs LaMalfa to run for his seat » Redding Record Searchlight |publisher=Redding.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-13}}</ref> LaMalfa won the primary election in June 2012 with 38% of the vote in an eight-person race and winning 10 out of the district's 11 counties.<ref>http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-primary/pdf/13-sov-summary.pdf</ref> On November 6, 2012, LaMalfa defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] candidate Jim Reed 57%–43%.<ref>http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2012-general/sov-complete.pdf</ref>

===2014 election===

In the 2014 Primary Election, LaMalfa faced criticism for accepting millions of dollars in farm subsidies, while supporting legislation to drastically cut food stamps. LaMalfa was challenged in the 2014 Congressional Election by Democrat Heidi Hall however Hall Lost reciveing less than 35% of the Vote.

====Tenure====
In 2013 LaMalfa signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any Global Warming legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>http://americansforprosperity.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/LaMalfa_Doug.pdf</ref>
