'''Curtis J. Clawson''' (born September 28, 1959) is an American politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[Florida's 19th congressional district]] since 2014. He is the former chief executive of Hayes Lemmerz, a Michigan-based automobile wheel and brakes supplier.<ref name="naples">{{cite news | url=http://www.naplesnews.com/business/exclusive-curt-clawson-business-record-congress-19 | title=NDN exclusive: Curt Clawson touts business acumen, but record is marred | work=[[Naples Daily News]] | date=12 March 2014 | accessdate=27 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=April 22, 2014 |title=Ex-C.E.O. Wins Florida Primary for House Seat |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/04/23/us/politics/ex-ceo-wins-florida-primary-for-house-seat.html |newspaper=New York Times |location=New York City |publisher=New York Times Company |accessdate=April 23, 2014}}</ref>

==Education and career==

Clawson attended [[Batesville High School (Indiana)|Batesville High School]] in [[Batesville, Indiana]].  A high school basketball star, he was recruited by [[Gene Keady]] for [[Purdue University]].<ref name="CJC"/>  At Purdue, he was a 2x All-Academic Big Ten selection (1982-83 & 1983-84).  He was a team captain for the 1983-84 Big Ten Champions, was a member of 2x NCAA teams (1982-83 & 1983-84) and an NIT Finalist team (1981–82).<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014"/>  He graduated in 1984 with a BA in Spanish and a BS from the [[Krannert School of Management]].<ref name="CJC"/>  He was named a "Purdue Old Master" in 2010 and received the Distinguished Alumni Award in 2014<ref name="CJC">[http://www.cla.purdue.edu/alumni/awards/distinguishedalumni/Recipients/Clawson.html Curtis J. Clawson : College of Liberal Arts : Purdue University<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

In 1990, he earned an MBA from [[Harvard University]].<ref name="CJC"/>

==U.S. House of Representatives==
===Elections===
;2014 special election
{{main|Florida's 19th congressional district special election, 2014}}
Clawson was the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] nominee in a [[Florida's 19th congressional district special election, 2014|special election]] to fill Florida's 19th district U.S. House of Representatives seat vacated by [[Trey Radel]].<ref name="WashPostSullivan04232014">Sullivan, Sean. [http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/04/22/curt-clawson-wins-republican-nomination-in-florida-special-election/ Curt Clawson wins Republican nomination in Florida special election], ''Washington Post'', April 23, 2014.</ref> and won the election on June 24, 2014. On April 22, 2014, Clawson defeated State Senate Majority Leader [[Lizbeth Benacquisto]] and former State Representative [[Paige Kreegel]] with 38% of the vote to Benacquisto's 26% and Kreegel's 25%.<ref name="WashPostSullivan04232014"/>  Clawson was endorsed in the primary by the [[Tea Party Express]].<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014">{{cite news |last=Leary |first=Alex |date=April 22, 2014 |title=Tea party candidate Curt Clawson wins Republican primary to replace former Rep. Trey Radel |url=http://www.tampabay.com/news/politics/national/tea-party-candidate-curt-clawson-wins-republican-primary-to-replace-former/2176397 |newspaper=Tampa Bay Times |location=Tampa |publisher=Times Publishing Company |accessdate=April 23, 2014 }}</ref>  He spent $2 million on advertising and in one of his ads he challenged U.S. President [[Barack Obama]] to a game of one on one basketball.<ref name="TampaTimesLeary04222014"/>

==Tenure==
During a House hearing on the July 24, 2014, Clawson mistook [[Nisha Desai Biswal]] and Arun M. Kumar, two senior U.S. officials of [[Indian American]] descent, for representatives of the [[India|Indian government]], saying, "I am familiar with your country, I love your country. Anything I can do to make the relationship with India better, I'm willing and enthusiastic about doing so. […] Just as your capital is welcome here to produce good-paying jobs in the U.S., I’d like our capital to be welcome there. I ask cooperation and commitment and priority from your government in so doing. Can I have that?"<ref>{{cite news|last1=Giri|first1=Nathan|title=Congressman Mistakes U.S. Officials For Indian Ones|url=http://time.com/3038010/congressman-curt-clawson-mistakes-u-s-officials-for-indian-officials/|accessdate=26 July 2014|agency=TIME}}</ref> He apologized for the gaffe the next day. “I made a mistake in speaking before being fully briefed and I apologize. I’m a quick study, but in this case I shot an air ball.” he said.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McCalmot|first1=Lucy|title=Curt Clawson sorry for nationality gaffe|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2014/07/curt-clawson-mistakes-staff-indians-109391.html|accessdate=26 July 2014}}</ref>
