'''Erik Paulsen''' (born May 14, 1965) is an American politician serving in the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|MN|3}} since 2009. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he previously served in the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] from 1995 to 2009 and as [[Minnesota House Majority Leader|Majority Leader]] from 2003 to 2007. His district in the western part of the [[Minneapolis-St. Paul|Twin Cities]] metropolitan area includes [[Bloomington, Minnesota|Bloomington]], [[Eden Prairie]], [[Edina, Minnesota|Edina]], [[Minnetonka]], [[Maple Grove, Minnesota|Maple Grove]] and [[Wayzata]].

== Early life, education, and career ==
Born in [[Bakersfield, California]], Paulsen graduated from [[Chaska High School]] in Minnesota in 1983.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us">http://www.leg.state.mn.us/legdb/fulldetail.aspx?id=10508</ref> He attended [[St. Olaf College]], and received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[mathematics]] in 1987. After college, Paulsen worked as an [[intern]] for Republican Senator [[Rudy Boschwitz]] from 1989 until Boschwitz was defeated by [[Democratic]] challenger [[Paul Wellstone]] in 1990.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> Paulsen then took a staff position with Republican Representative [[Jim Ramstad]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]. He worked on Ramstad's local congressional campaign in 1992 before seeking election to the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] in 1994.<ref name="legistorm.com">http://www.legistorm.com/blog/2008-election-big-for-former-congressional-staffers.html</ref><ref>[http://www.leg.mn/legdb/fulldetail.asp?ID=10508 Minnesota Legislative Library for Erik Paulsen]</ref> 

From 2007 to 2009, Paulsen worked as a part-time business analyst for [[Target Corporation]] while a member of the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]].<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/>

== Minnesota legislature ==
Paulsen was initially elected as an [[Republican Party of Minnesota#Independent-Republican era|Independent Republican]]. He listed no occupation in 1994.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> In 1996 he authored a [[Minnesota constitution|constitutional amendment]] to establish a 12-year term limit for state senators and representatives. Paulsen, however, served 14 years before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives.<ref name="legistorm.com"/><ref>https://www.revisor.mn.gov/bills/status_result.php?body=House&session=0801997&author1[]=&legid1=10508</ref> He served on the Commerce and Labor, Rules and Legislative Administration, Taxes, and Ways and Means committees.<ref name="protege">{{cite news|last=Duchschere|first=Kevin|title=Ramstad protégé Paulsen announces House bid|url=http://www.startribune.com/local/south/14473002.html|newspaper=[[Star Tribune]]|date=January 27, 2008}}</ref>

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2008
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Minnesota, 2008#District 3}}
Paulsen won a three-way race for Minnesota's Third Congressional District in November 2008.<ref>{{cite news|last=Brunswick|first=Mark|last2=Furst|first2=Randy|title=Paulsen triumphs over Madia for Third District seat|url=http://www.startribune.com/politics/national/house/33872049.html|newspaper=[[Star Tribune]]|date=November 5, 2008}}</ref> His U.S. House candidacy was announced after the incumbent, [[Jim Ramstad]] (a Republican), announced his retirement in 2007, which gave an opportunity for both major parties to field potential candidates. Shortly after he announced his retirement, Ramstad endorsed Paulsen and served as the chairman of Paulsen's Steering Committee.<ref>Ramstad endorses Erik Paulsen. [http://www.paulsenforcongress.com/index.asp?Type=B_EV&SEC={988B9D2B-ACA9-49D0-937D-B4C8640D009B}&DE={4E00262A-C198-41C3-8C0C-699D96BE2997}] {{dead link|date=October 2010}}</ref> Paulsen was a speaker at the [[2008 Republican National Convention]] in [[Saint Paul, Minnesota]].

Paulsen won the election with 48.48% of the vote, to [[Democratic-Farmer-Labor Party]] nominee [[Ashwin Madia]]'s 40.85% and  [[Independence Party of Minnesota]] candidate David Dillon's 10.56%. While not achieving a majority, Paulsen defeated Madia by about 30,000 votes.<ref>[http://electionresults.sos.state.mn.us/20081104/ElecRslts.asp?M=CG&CD=03 Minnesota Secretary of State]</ref>

;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Minnesota, 2010#District 3}}
Paulson won reelection with 59% of the vote against [[Democratic-Farmer-Labor Party]] challenger [[Jim Meffert]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.cnn.com/ELECTION/2010/results/state/#MN | work=CNN | title=State Results - Election Center 2010 - Elections & Politics from CNN.com}}</ref> 

During the race, Meffert filed a complaint with the [[United States House Committee on Ethics]] claiming that Paulsen distributed a deliberately misleading mailing to his constituents using the franking privilege afforded to House members. The committee has yet to act upon the complaint.<ref>{{cite news|last=Wallbank|first=Derek|url=http://www.minnpost.com/derekwallbank/2011/04/28/27809/no_comment_the_story_of_an_ethics_complaint_and_what_didnt_happen_next | work=MinnPost|title=No Comment: The story of an ethics complaint and what didn't happen next|date=April 28, 2011}}.</ref>

==Political positions==
{{Prose|date=May 2010}}
Paulsen supports continuing [[Jobs and Growth Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2003|Bush-era tax cuts]] and global [[free trade]] agreements.<ref>Black, Eric. [http://www.minnpost.com/ericblack/2008/10/15/3888/erik_paulsen_on_tax_cuts_inflammatory_and_misleading Erik Paulsen on tax cuts: inflammatory and misleading.] ''Minn Post.'' 15 October 2008.</ref>

Paulsen opposes a [[Public health insurance option|public health care option]], saying it would represent a "government takeover" of health care. Instead, he supported a [http://docs.house.gov/rules/3962/RepublicanAlternative3962_9.pdf Republican alternative plan].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.minnpost.com/stories/2009/06/30/9908/minnesota_delegations_positions_on_health_care_plans |title=Minnesota delegation's positions on health care plans |publisher=MinnPost |date=2009-06-30 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref> At an April 7, 2010, GOP rally in Minneapolis, Paulsen described the recently enacted health care reform law as a "government takeover of health care," a claim that [[Minnesota Public Radio]] states "isn't correct." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2010/04/07/palin-bachmann-rally-fact-check/ |title=Fact-checking yesterday's political rallies &#124; Minnesota Public Radio NewsQ |publisher=Minnesota.publicradio.org |date=2010-04-08 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

Paulsen has called for an end to Minnesota's ban on building [[nuclear power]] plants, saying that "trying to meet our energy needs without using nuclear energy is a little bit like trying to row a boat with one oar."<ref>[http://www.keyc.com/node/30600 Walz, Paulsen Tout Nuclear Power.] ''KEYC News.'' 24 November 2009.</ref><ref>Bakst, Brian. [http://abcnews.go.com/Business/wireStory?id=9166100 Effort to Scrap Anti-Nuclear Law in Minn. Ramps Up.] ''[[Associated Press]].'' 24 November 2009.</ref>
 
Paulsen voted against the [[American Clean Energy and Security Act]] (2009), an effort to curb emissions of [[greenhouse gases]] that cause [[climate change]].<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll477.xml HR 2454: American Clean Energy and Security Act] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen voted against the [[American Recovery and Reinvestment Act]],<ref>[https://votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=23644&can_id=3833 Appropriations, Tax Law Amendments, and Unemployment Benefit Amendments ("Stimulus Bill")] ''Project Vote Smart''.</ref> citing its high cost to current and future taxpayers.{{Citation needed|date=July 2010}} 

Paulsen voted against The Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2009.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll968.xml The Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2009] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen voted against a bill repealing the U.S. military's "[[don't ask, don't tell]]" policy in favor of allowing individuals who have [[Coming out|openly acknowledged their homosexuality]] to serve.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2010/roll317.xml Roll Call 317] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref> He voted against several employment discrimination law amendments in 2009, as well as a bill that would have, among other intentions, expanded the definition of hate crimes to include “felonies motivated by prejudice based on national origin, sexual orientation, or gender identity of the victim.” <ref>http://www.votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=26244&can_id=3833</ref> He voted in support of required background checks for school athletic coaches.<ref name="votesmart.org">http://www.votesmart.org/voting_category.php?can_id=3833</ref>

Paulsen voted repeatedly in 2010 against extending benefits to unemployed Americans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=30588&can_id=3833 |title=Project Vote Smart - Representative Paulsen on HR 4213 - Unemployment Benefits Extension and Tax Law Amendments |publisher=Votesmart.org |date= |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

Paulsen opposed the [[Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009|Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act]] intended to prevent pay discrimination against women.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll037.xml Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen has voted multiple times in favor of prohibiting public or federal funding for abortion services. He voted for the Seifert Amendment, which, though rejected, worked to prohibit grants for groups associated with clinics and other establishments that provide abortions.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen opposed two proposed smoking bans in 2007.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen voted against a resolution telling the president to remove troops and armed forces from Pakistan.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen introduced the Text a Tip Act to the House in 2010. The bill would have allowed users to send tips about crimes to a third party, which would have removed all identifying information about the user before forwarding the message to the police.<ref>http://www.votesmart.org/speech_detail.php?sc_id=590459&keyword=&phrase=&contain=</ref> The bill died in committee and was not adopted.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h111-5913</ref>

Paulsen cosponsored a draft of the Small Business Assistance and Relief Act in 2010, to provide increased lending and aid for small businesses and ease their financial encumbrances.<ref>http://www.votesmart.org/speech_detail.php?sc_id=590458&keyword=&phrase=&contain=</ref><ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/billtext.xpd?bill=h111-5554</ref>

Paulsen voted against a bill to fund medical treatment for 9/11 first responders and victims.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/111/house/2/550 | work=The New York Times | title=House Vote 550 - Passes 9/11 Health Care Bill}}</ref>

Paulsen voted in favor of the Federal budget plan for fiscal 2012 that, among other provisions, provided for substantial overhaul of the Medicare program including replacement of the traditional program with a premium support payment for private health insurance coverage for Americans currently under age 55.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/vote.xpd?vote=h2011-277</ref>

On July 24, 2013, Paulsen voted to continue funding NSA surveillance of all U.S. citizens.<ref>http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2013/roll412.xml</ref> which at least one court has ruled is a violation of Americans' Fourth Amendment rights.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/2006/08/17/judge-rules-terrorist-surveillance-program-unconstitutional/ | work=Fox News | title=Judge Rules Terrorist Surveillance Program Unconstitutional | date=2006-08-17}}</ref>

===Legislation===
On February 13, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[National Park Service 100th Anniversary Commemorative Coin Act (H.R. 627; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] to mint and issue [[gold]], [[silver]], and [[Half dollar (United States coin)|half-dollar]] clad coins in commemoration of the 100th anniversary of the establishment of the [[National Park Service]] (NPS).<ref name=627sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 627 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/627|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=1 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=Pekow1>{{cite news|last=Pekow|first=Charles|title=House approves commemorative coins for National Park Service centennial|url=|accessdate=1 May 2014|newspaper=The Examiner|date=30 April 2014}}</ref> The coins would all have a surcharge attached, the money from which would be given to the [[National Park Foundation]].<ref name="Pekow1"/> Paulsen argued that "even during tough economic times, it's important to find new, cost-effective ways to preserve these treasures for future generations to learn from and enjoy."<ref name=PaulsenPRfeb13>{{cite web|title=Paulsen Introduces Legislation to Celebrate 100th Anniversary of National Park Service|url=http://paulsen.house.gov/press-releases/paulsen-introduces-legislation-to-celebrate-100th-anniversary-of-national-park-service/|publisher=House Office of Erik Paulsen|accessdate=1 May 2014|date=13 February 2013}}</ref> Paulsen called the National Parks "some of our most treasured natural resources."<ref name="PaulsenPRfeb13"/>

Paulsen strongly supported the [[Money Remittances Improvement Act of 2014 (H.R. 4386; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would make it easier for [[Non-bank financial institution|nonbank financial institutions]] such as [[Money services business|money service businesses]] to provide [[remittance]] payments internationally.<ref name=allAfricaMay7>{{cite news|last=Radio|first=Dalsan|title=Somalia: Congressman Ellison's Money Remittances Improvement Act Passed|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201405071137.html|accessdate=8 May 2014|newspaper=All Africa|date=7 May 2014}}</ref> Paulsen argued that the bill would make it easier for American immigrants "supporting their extended families overseas" to help their relatives, while still "providing the necessary safeguards to ensure their money reaches its intended destination."<ref name=EllisonPRMay6>{{cite web|title=Ellison, Paulsen, Duffy Applaud Passage of Money Remittances Improvement Act|url=http://ellison.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/ellison-paulsen-duffy-applaud-passage-of-money-remittances-improvement|publisher=House Office of Keith Ellison|accessdate=8 May 2014|date=6 May 2014}}</ref> The bill would help these people by "streamlining the remittance process and eliminating regulatory barriers to sending money home."<ref name="EllisonPRMay6"/>

On November 21, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[Stop Exploitation Through Trafficking Act of 2013 (H.R. 3610; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would require each state, within three years, to have in effect legislation that: (1) treats a minor who has engaged or attempted to engage in a commercial sex act as a victim of a severe form of [[human trafficking|trafficking in persons]], (2) discourages the charging or prosecution of such an individual for a [[prostitution]] or sex trafficking offense, and (3) encourages the diversion of such individual to [[Child Protective Services|child protection services]].<ref name=3610sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3610 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3610|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=19 May 2014}}</ref> The bill was scheduled to be voted on in the House on May 20, 2014 under a [[suspension of the rules]].<ref name=MarcosMay16>{{cite news|last=Marcos|first=Cristina|title=Next week: Lawmakers to debate defense and drones|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/scheduling/206363-next-week-defense-authorization-drones|accessdate=19 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=16 May 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
During college at St. Olaf, Paulsen met his wife, Kelly. The Paulsens had four daughters as of 2014, and live in [[Eden Prairie, Minnesota|Eden Prairie]]. Paulsen serves as a Board Trustee of the Minneapolis Institute of Arts, and as a board member of the Eden Prairie A Brighter Day Foundation, Habitat for Global Learning, Habitat for Technology and the Southdale YMCA. He is a member of the American Council of Young Political Leaders and the Eden Prairie Chamber of Commerce, and volunteers for Learning Exchange.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=3833 |title=Project Vote Smart - Representative Erik Paulsen - Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org |date=1965-05-14 |accessdate=2010-11-29}}</ref>

;Fellowships, honors, and recognitions
Paulsen has participated in the inaugural two-year class of the Aspen Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, the German Marshall Memorial Fellowship, the Young Leaders Forum of the National Committee on U.S.-China Relations, and the American Council of Young Political Leaders.<ref name="house1">http://paulsen.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=98&sectiontree-2,98</ref> He has been granted an [[Aspen Institute]] Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, and a [[German Marshall Fund|Marshall Memorial Fellowship]] from the German Marshall Fund of the United States.

==Political positions==
{{Prose|date=May 2010}}
Paulsen supports continuing [[Jobs and Growth Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2003|Bush-era tax cuts]] and global [[free trade]] agreements.<ref>Black, Eric. [http://www.minnpost.com/ericblack/2008/10/15/3888/erik_paulsen_on_tax_cuts_inflammatory_and_misleading Erik Paulsen on tax cuts: inflammatory and misleading.] ''Minn Post.'' 15 October 2008.</ref>

Paulsen opposes a [[Public health insurance option|public health care option]], saying it would represent a "government takeover" of health care. Instead, he supported a [http://docs.house.gov/rules/3962/RepublicanAlternative3962_9.pdf Republican alternative plan].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.minnpost.com/stories/2009/06/30/9908/minnesota_delegations_positions_on_health_care_plans |title=Minnesota delegation's positions on health care plans |publisher=MinnPost |date=2009-06-30 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref> At an April 7, 2010, GOP rally in Minneapolis, Paulsen described the recently enacted health care reform law as a "government takeover of health care," a claim that [[Minnesota Public Radio]] states "isn't correct." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2010/04/07/palin-bachmann-rally-fact-check/ |title=Fact-checking yesterday's political rallies &#124; Minnesota Public Radio NewsQ |publisher=Minnesota.publicradio.org |date=2010-04-08 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

Paulsen has called for an end to Minnesota's ban on building [[nuclear power]] plants, saying that "trying to meet our energy needs without using nuclear energy is a little bit like trying to row a boat with one oar."<ref>[http://www.keyc.com/node/30600 Walz, Paulsen Tout Nuclear Power.] ''KEYC News.'' 24 November 2009.</ref><ref>Bakst, Brian. [http://abcnews.go.com/Business/wireStory?id=9166100 Effort to Scrap Anti-Nuclear Law in Minn. Ramps Up.] ''[[Associated Press]].'' 24 November 2009.</ref>
 
Paulsen voted against the [[American Clean Energy and Security Act]] (2009), an effort to curb emissions of [[greenhouse gases]] that cause [[climate change]].<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll477.xml HR 2454: American Clean Energy and Security Act] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen voted against the [[American Recovery and Reinvestment Act]],<ref>[https://votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=23644&can_id=3833 Appropriations, Tax Law Amendments, and Unemployment Benefit Amendments ("Stimulus Bill")] ''Project Vote Smart''.</ref> citing its high cost to current and future taxpayers.{{Citation needed|date=July 2010}} 

Paulsen voted against The Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2009.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll968.xml The Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2009] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen voted against a bill repealing the U.S. military's "[[don't ask, don't tell]]" policy in favor of allowing individuals who have [[Coming out|openly acknowledged their homosexuality]] to serve.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2010/roll317.xml Roll Call 317] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref> He voted against several employment discrimination law amendments in 2009, as well as a bill that would have, among other intentions, expanded the definition of hate crimes to include “felonies motivated by prejudice based on national origin, sexual orientation, or gender identity of the victim.” <ref>http://www.votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=26244&can_id=3833</ref> He voted in support of required background checks for school athletic coaches.<ref name="votesmart.org">http://www.votesmart.org/voting_category.php?can_id=3833</ref>

Paulsen voted repeatedly in 2010 against extending benefits to unemployed Americans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/issue_keyvote_detail.php?cs_id=30588&can_id=3833 |title=Project Vote Smart - Representative Paulsen on HR 4213 - Unemployment Benefits Extension and Tax Law Amendments |publisher=Votesmart.org |date= |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

Paulsen opposed the [[Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009|Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act]] intended to prevent pay discrimination against women.<ref>[http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2009/roll037.xml Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009] Office of the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives</ref>

Paulsen has voted multiple times in favor of prohibiting public or federal funding for abortion services. He voted for the Seifert Amendment, which, though rejected, worked to prohibit grants for groups associated with clinics and other establishments that provide abortions.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen opposed two proposed smoking bans in 2007.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen voted against a resolution telling the president to remove troops and armed forces from Pakistan.<ref name="votesmart.org"/>

Paulsen introduced the Text a Tip Act to the House in 2010. The bill would have allowed users to send tips about crimes to a third party, which would have removed all identifying information about the user before forwarding the message to the police.<ref>http://www.votesmart.org/speech_detail.php?sc_id=590459&keyword=&phrase=&contain=</ref> The bill died in committee and was not adopted.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h111-5913</ref>

Paulsen cosponsored a draft of the Small Business Assistance and Relief Act in 2010, to provide increased lending and aid for small businesses and ease their financial encumbrances.<ref>http://www.votesmart.org/speech_detail.php?sc_id=590458&keyword=&phrase=&contain=</ref><ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/billtext.xpd?bill=h111-5554</ref>

Paulsen voted against a bill to fund medical treatment for 9/11 first responders and victims.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/111/house/2/550 | work=The New York Times | title=House Vote 550 - Passes 9/11 Health Care Bill}}</ref>

Paulsen voted in favor of the Federal budget plan for fiscal 2012 that, among other provisions, provided for substantial overhaul of the Medicare program including replacement of the traditional program with a premium support payment for private health insurance coverage for Americans currently under age 55.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/vote.xpd?vote=h2011-277</ref>

On July 24, 2013, Paulsen voted to continue funding NSA surveillance of all U.S. citizens.<ref>http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2013/roll412.xml</ref> which at least one court has ruled is a violation of Americans' Fourth Amendment rights.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/2006/08/17/judge-rules-terrorist-surveillance-program-unconstitutional/ | work=Fox News | title=Judge Rules Terrorist Surveillance Program Unconstitutional | date=2006-08-17}}</ref>

===Legislation===
On February 13, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[National Park Service 100th Anniversary Commemorative Coin Act (H.R. 627; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] to mint and issue [[gold]], [[silver]], and [[Half dollar (United States coin)|half-dollar]] clad coins in commemoration of the 100th anniversary of the establishment of the [[National Park Service]] (NPS).<ref name=627sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 627 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/627|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=1 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=Pekow1>{{cite news|last=Pekow|first=Charles|title=House approves commemorative coins for National Park Service centennial|url=|accessdate=1 May 2014|newspaper=The Examiner|date=30 April 2014}}</ref> The coins would all have a surcharge attached, the money from which would be given to the [[National Park Foundation]].<ref name="Pekow1"/> Paulsen argued that "even during tough economic times, it's important to find new, cost-effective ways to preserve these treasures for future generations to learn from and enjoy."<ref name=PaulsenPRfeb13>{{cite web|title=Paulsen Introduces Legislation to Celebrate 100th Anniversary of National Park Service|url=http://paulsen.house.gov/press-releases/paulsen-introduces-legislation-to-celebrate-100th-anniversary-of-national-park-service/|publisher=House Office of Erik Paulsen|accessdate=1 May 2014|date=13 February 2013}}</ref> Paulsen called the National Parks "some of our most treasured natural resources."<ref name="PaulsenPRfeb13"/>

Paulsen strongly supported the [[Money Remittances Improvement Act of 2014 (H.R. 4386; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would make it easier for [[Non-bank financial institution|nonbank financial institutions]] such as [[Money services business|money service businesses]] to provide [[remittance]] payments internationally.<ref name=allAfricaMay7>{{cite news|last=Radio|first=Dalsan|title=Somalia: Congressman Ellison's Money Remittances Improvement Act Passed|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201405071137.html|accessdate=8 May 2014|newspaper=All Africa|date=7 May 2014}}</ref> Paulsen argued that the bill would make it easier for American immigrants "supporting their extended families overseas" to help their relatives, while still "providing the necessary safeguards to ensure their money reaches its intended destination."<ref name=EllisonPRMay6>{{cite web|title=Ellison, Paulsen, Duffy Applaud Passage of Money Remittances Improvement Act|url=http://ellison.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/ellison-paulsen-duffy-applaud-passage-of-money-remittances-improvement|publisher=House Office of Keith Ellison|accessdate=8 May 2014|date=6 May 2014}}</ref> The bill would help these people by "streamlining the remittance process and eliminating regulatory barriers to sending money home."<ref name="EllisonPRMay6"/>

On November 21, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[Stop Exploitation Through Trafficking Act of 2013 (H.R. 3610; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would require each state, within three years, to have in effect legislation that: (1) treats a minor who has engaged or attempted to engage in a commercial sex act as a victim of a severe form of [[human trafficking|trafficking in persons]], (2) discourages the charging or prosecution of such an individual for a [[prostitution]] or sex trafficking offense, and (3) encourages the diversion of such individual to [[Child Protective Services|child protection services]].<ref name=3610sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3610 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3610|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=19 May 2014}}</ref> The bill was scheduled to be voted on in the House on May 20, 2014 under a [[suspension of the rules]].<ref name=MarcosMay16>{{cite news|last=Marcos|first=Cristina|title=Next week: Lawmakers to debate defense and drones|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/scheduling/206363-next-week-defense-authorization-drones|accessdate=19 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=16 May 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
During college at St. Olaf, Paulsen met his wife, Kelly. The Paulsens had four daughters as of 2014, and live in [[Eden Prairie, Minnesota|Eden Prairie]]. Paulsen serves as a Board Trustee of the Minneapolis Institute of Arts, and as a board member of the Eden Prairie A Brighter Day Foundation, Habitat for Global Learning, Habitat for Technology and the Southdale YMCA. He is a member of the American Council of Young Political Leaders and the Eden Prairie Chamber of Commerce, and volunteers for Learning Exchange.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=3833 |title=Project Vote Smart - Representative Erik Paulsen - Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org |date=1965-05-14 |accessdate=2010-11-29}}</ref>

;Fellowships, honors, and recognitions
Paulsen has participated in the inaugural two-year class of the Aspen Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, the German Marshall Memorial Fellowship, the Young Leaders Forum of the National Committee on U.S.-China Relations, and the American Council of Young Political Leaders.<ref name="house1">http://paulsen.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=98&sectiontree-2,98</ref> He has been granted an [[Aspen Institute]] Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, and a [[German Marshall Fund|Marshall Memorial Fellowship]] from the German Marshall Fund of the United States.

'''Erik Paulsen''' (born May 14, 1965) is an American politician serving in the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|MN|3}} since 2009. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he previously served in the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] from 1995 to 2009 and as [[Minnesota House Majority Leader|Majority Leader]] from 2003 to 2007. His district in the western part of the [[Minneapolis-St. Paul|Twin Cities]] metropolitan area includes [[Bloomington, Minnesota|Bloomington]], [[Eden Prairie]], [[Edina, Minnesota|Edina]], [[Minnetonka]], [[Maple Grove, Minnesota|Maple Grove]] and [[Wayzata]].

== Early life, education, and career ==
Born in [[Bakersfield, California]], Paulsen graduated from [[Chaska High School]] in Minnesota in 1983.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us">http://www.leg.state.mn.us/legdb/fulldetail.aspx?id=10508</ref> He attended [[St. Olaf College]], and received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[mathematics]] in 1987. After college, Paulsen worked as an [[intern]] for Republican Senator [[Rudy Boschwitz]] from 1989 until Boschwitz was defeated by [[Democratic]] challenger [[Paul Wellstone]] in 1990.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> Paulsen then took a staff position with Republican Representative [[Jim Ramstad]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]. He worked on Ramstad's local congressional campaign in 1992 before seeking election to the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] in 1994.<ref name="legistorm.com">http://www.legistorm.com/blog/2008-election-big-for-former-congressional-staffers.html</ref><ref>[http://www.leg.mn/legdb/fulldetail.asp?ID=10508 Minnesota Legislative Library for Erik Paulsen]</ref> 

From 2007 to 2009, Paulsen worked as a part-time business analyst for [[Target Corporation]] while a member of the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]].<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/>

== Minnesota legislature ==
Paulsen was initially elected as an [[Republican Party of Minnesota#Independent-Republican era|Independent Republican]]. He listed no occupation in 1994.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> In 1996 he authored a [[Minnesota constitution|constitutional amendment]] to establish a 12-year term limit for state senators and representatives. Paulsen, however, served 14 years before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives.<ref name="legistorm.com"/><ref>https://www.revisor.mn.gov/bills/status_result.php?body=House&session=0801997&author1[]=&legid1=10508</ref> He served on the Commerce and Labor, Rules and Legislative Administration, Taxes, and Ways and Means committees.<ref name="protege">{{cite news|last=Duchschere|first=Kevin|title=Ramstad protégé Paulsen announces House bid|url=http://www.startribune.com/local/south/14473002.html|newspaper=[[Star Tribune]]|date=January 27, 2008}}</ref>

Paulsen cosponsored a draft of the Small Business Assistance and Relief Act in 2010, to provide increased lending and aid for small businesses and ease their financial encumbrances.<ref>http://www.votesmart.org/speech_detail.php?sc_id=590458&keyword=&phrase=&contain=</ref><ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/billtext.xpd?bill=h111-5554</ref>

Paulsen voted against a bill to fund medical treatment for 9/11 first responders and victims.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/111/house/2/550 | work=The New York Times | title=House Vote 550 - Passes 9/11 Health Care Bill}}</ref>

Paulsen voted in favor of the Federal budget plan for fiscal 2012 that, among other provisions, provided for substantial overhaul of the Medicare program including replacement of the traditional program with a premium support payment for private health insurance coverage for Americans currently under age 55.<ref>http://www.govtrack.us/congress/vote.xpd?vote=h2011-277</ref>

On July 24, 2013, Paulsen voted to continue funding NSA surveillance of all U.S. citizens.<ref>http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2013/roll412.xml</ref> which at least one court has ruled is a violation of Americans' Fourth Amendment rights.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/2006/08/17/judge-rules-terrorist-surveillance-program-unconstitutional/ | work=Fox News | title=Judge Rules Terrorist Surveillance Program Unconstitutional | date=2006-08-17}}</ref>

===Legislation===
On February 13, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[National Park Service 100th Anniversary Commemorative Coin Act (H.R. 627; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would direct the [[United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] to mint and issue [[gold]], [[silver]], and [[Half dollar (United States coin)|half-dollar]] clad coins in commemoration of the 100th anniversary of the establishment of the [[National Park Service]] (NPS).<ref name=627sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 627 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/627|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=1 May 2014}}</ref><ref name=Pekow1>{{cite news|last=Pekow|first=Charles|title=House approves commemorative coins for National Park Service centennial|url=|accessdate=1 May 2014|newspaper=The Examiner|date=30 April 2014}}</ref> The coins would all have a surcharge attached, the money from which would be given to the [[National Park Foundation]].<ref name="Pekow1"/> Paulsen argued that "even during tough economic times, it's important to find new, cost-effective ways to preserve these treasures for future generations to learn from and enjoy."<ref name=PaulsenPRfeb13>{{cite web|title=Paulsen Introduces Legislation to Celebrate 100th Anniversary of National Park Service|url=http://paulsen.house.gov/press-releases/paulsen-introduces-legislation-to-celebrate-100th-anniversary-of-national-park-service/|publisher=House Office of Erik Paulsen|accessdate=1 May 2014|date=13 February 2013}}</ref> Paulsen called the National Parks "some of our most treasured natural resources."<ref name="PaulsenPRfeb13"/>

Paulsen strongly supported the [[Money Remittances Improvement Act of 2014 (H.R. 4386; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would make it easier for [[Non-bank financial institution|nonbank financial institutions]] such as [[Money services business|money service businesses]] to provide [[remittance]] payments internationally.<ref name=allAfricaMay7>{{cite news|last=Radio|first=Dalsan|title=Somalia: Congressman Ellison's Money Remittances Improvement Act Passed|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201405071137.html|accessdate=8 May 2014|newspaper=All Africa|date=7 May 2014}}</ref> Paulsen argued that the bill would make it easier for American immigrants "supporting their extended families overseas" to help their relatives, while still "providing the necessary safeguards to ensure their money reaches its intended destination."<ref name=EllisonPRMay6>{{cite web|title=Ellison, Paulsen, Duffy Applaud Passage of Money Remittances Improvement Act|url=http://ellison.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/ellison-paulsen-duffy-applaud-passage-of-money-remittances-improvement|publisher=House Office of Keith Ellison|accessdate=8 May 2014|date=6 May 2014}}</ref> The bill would help these people by "streamlining the remittance process and eliminating regulatory barriers to sending money home."<ref name="EllisonPRMay6"/>

On November 21, 2013, Paulsen introduced the [[Stop Exploitation Through Trafficking Act of 2013 (H.R. 3610; 113th Congress)]], a bill that would require each state, within three years, to have in effect legislation that: (1) treats a minor who has engaged or attempted to engage in a commercial sex act as a victim of a severe form of [[human trafficking|trafficking in persons]], (2) discourages the charging or prosecution of such an individual for a [[prostitution]] or sex trafficking offense, and (3) encourages the diversion of such individual to [[Child Protective Services|child protection services]].<ref name=3610sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3610 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/3610|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=19 May 2014}}</ref> The bill was scheduled to be voted on in the House on May 20, 2014 under a [[suspension of the rules]].<ref name=MarcosMay16>{{cite news|last=Marcos|first=Cristina|title=Next week: Lawmakers to debate defense and drones|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/scheduling/206363-next-week-defense-authorization-drones|accessdate=19 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=16 May 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
During college at St. Olaf, Paulsen met his wife, Kelly. The Paulsens had four daughters as of 2014, and live in [[Eden Prairie, Minnesota|Eden Prairie]]. Paulsen serves as a Board Trustee of the Minneapolis Institute of Arts, and as a board member of the Eden Prairie A Brighter Day Foundation, Habitat for Global Learning, Habitat for Technology and the Southdale YMCA. He is a member of the American Council of Young Political Leaders and the Eden Prairie Chamber of Commerce, and volunteers for Learning Exchange.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=3833 |title=Project Vote Smart - Representative Erik Paulsen - Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org |date=1965-05-14 |accessdate=2010-11-29}}</ref>

'''Erik Paulsen''' (born May 14, 1965) is an American politician serving in the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|MN|3}} since 2009. A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he previously served in the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] from 1995 to 2009 and as [[Minnesota House Majority Leader|Majority Leader]] from 2003 to 2007. His district in the western part of the [[Minneapolis-St. Paul|Twin Cities]] metropolitan area includes [[Bloomington, Minnesota|Bloomington]], [[Eden Prairie]], [[Edina, Minnesota|Edina]], [[Minnetonka]], [[Maple Grove, Minnesota|Maple Grove]] and [[Wayzata]].

== Early life, education, and career ==
Born in [[Bakersfield, California]], Paulsen graduated from [[Chaska High School]] in Minnesota in 1983.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us">http://www.leg.state.mn.us/legdb/fulldetail.aspx?id=10508</ref> He attended [[St. Olaf College]], and received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[mathematics]] in 1987. After college, Paulsen worked as an [[intern]] for Republican Senator [[Rudy Boschwitz]] from 1989 until Boschwitz was defeated by [[Democratic]] challenger [[Paul Wellstone]] in 1990.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> Paulsen then took a staff position with Republican Representative [[Jim Ramstad]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]. He worked on Ramstad's local congressional campaign in 1992 before seeking election to the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] in 1994.<ref name="legistorm.com">http://www.legistorm.com/blog/2008-election-big-for-former-congressional-staffers.html</ref><ref>[http://www.leg.mn/legdb/fulldetail.asp?ID=10508 Minnesota Legislative Library for Erik Paulsen]</ref> 

From 2007 to 2009, Paulsen worked as a part-time business analyst for [[Target Corporation]] while a member of the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]].<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/>

== Minnesota legislature ==
Paulsen was initially elected as an [[Republican Party of Minnesota#Independent-Republican era|Independent Republican]]. He listed no occupation in 1994.<ref name="leg.state.mn.us"/> In 1996 he authored a [[Minnesota constitution|constitutional amendment]] to establish a 12-year term limit for state senators and representatives. Paulsen, however, served 14 years before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives.<ref name="legistorm.com"/><ref>https://www.revisor.mn.gov/bills/status_result.php?body=House&session=0801997&author1[]=&legid1=10508</ref> He served on the Commerce and Labor, Rules and Legislative Administration, Taxes, and Ways and Means committees.<ref name="protege">{{cite news|last=Duchschere|first=Kevin|title=Ramstad protégé Paulsen announces House bid|url=http://www.startribune.com/local/south/14473002.html|newspaper=[[Star Tribune]]|date=January 27, 2008}}</ref>

Paulsen won a three-way race for Minnesota's Third Congressional District in November 2008.<ref>{{cite news|last=Brunswick|first=Mark|last2=Furst|first2=Randy|title=Paulsen triumphs over Madia for Third District seat|url=http://www.startribune.com/politics/national/house/33872049.html|newspaper=[[Star Tribune]]|date=November 5, 2008}}</ref> His U.S. House candidacy was announced after the incumbent, [[Jim Ramstad]] (a Republican), announced his retirement in 2007, which gave an opportunity for both major parties to field potential candidates. Shortly after he announced his retirement, Ramstad endorsed Paulsen and served as the chairman of Paulsen's Steering Committee.<ref>Ramstad endorses Erik Paulsen. [http://www.paulsenforcongress.com/index.asp?Type=B_EV&SEC={988B9D2B-ACA9-49D0-937D-B4C8640D009B}&DE={4E00262A-C198-41C3-8C0C-699D96BE2997}] {{dead link|date=October 2010}}</ref> Paulsen was a speaker at the [[2008 Republican National Convention]] in [[Saint Paul, Minnesota]].

Paulsen won the election with 48.48% of the vote, to [[Democratic-Farmer-Labor Party]] nominee [[Ashwin Madia]]'s 40.85% and  [[Independence Party of Minnesota]] candidate David Dillon's 10.56%. While not achieving a majority, Paulsen defeated Madia by about 30,000 votes.<ref>[http://electionresults.sos.state.mn.us/20081104/ElecRslts.asp?M=CG&CD=03 Minnesota Secretary of State]</ref>

Paulsen opposes a [[Public health insurance option|public health care option]], saying it would represent a "government takeover" of health care. Instead, he supported a [http://docs.house.gov/rules/3962/RepublicanAlternative3962_9.pdf Republican alternative plan].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.minnpost.com/stories/2009/06/30/9908/minnesota_delegations_positions_on_health_care_plans |title=Minnesota delegation's positions on health care plans |publisher=MinnPost |date=2009-06-30 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref> At an April 7, 2010, GOP rally in Minneapolis, Paulsen described the recently enacted health care reform law as a "government takeover of health care," a claim that [[Minnesota Public Radio]] states "isn't correct." <ref>{{cite web|url=http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2010/04/07/palin-bachmann-rally-fact-check/ |title=Fact-checking yesterday's political rallies &#124; Minnesota Public Radio NewsQ |publisher=Minnesota.publicradio.org |date=2010-04-08 |accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>

Paulsen has participated in the inaugural two-year class of the Aspen Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, the German Marshall Memorial Fellowship, the Young Leaders Forum of the National Committee on U.S.-China Relations, and the American Council of Young Political Leaders.<ref name="house1">http://paulsen.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=98&sectiontree-2,98</ref> He has been granted an [[Aspen Institute]] Rodel Fellowship in Public Leadership, and a [[German Marshall Fund|Marshall Memorial Fellowship]] from the German Marshall Fund of the United States.
