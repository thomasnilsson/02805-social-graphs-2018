'''Joseph "Joe" Courtney''' (born April 6, 1953) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|Connecticut|2}}, serving since 2007.  The district includes most of the eastern third of the state, including [[Norwich, Connecticut|Norwich]] and [[New London, Connecticut|New London]].  He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].

==Early life and education ==

Courtney grew up in suburban Hartford. In 1975 he graduated from [[Tufts University]]. He earned a law degree from the [[University of Connecticut School of Law]] in 1978.

== Career ==

=== Prior to Congress ===
After graduating from law school, Courtney worked as a public defender for three years. He became a partner in the law firm Courtney, Boyan & Foran, and also served as Town Attorney in [[Vernon, Connecticut]], where he lives.

From 1987 to 1994, Courtney served in the [[Connecticut House of Representatives]], representing Connecticut's 56th district. He was chairman of the Public Health and Human Service Committee and oversaw the Blue Ribbon Commission on Universal Health Insurance. In 1994, he was honored by [[Connecticut Magazine]] for his bipartisan efforts in the state house.

In 1998, after four years out of office, Courtney made an unsuccessful bid for [[Lieutenant Governor of Connecticut|Lieutenant Governor]]. In 2002, he ran for Congress against incumbent Republican [[Rob Simmons]]. Courtney did not announce his candidacy or raise money until September 2001; by that time, Simmons had more than $500,000 in campaign funding. Simmons defeated Courtney in the November election, 54% to 46%.<ref>{{cite news |title=House Rules|newspaper=Washington Post |author= Michael Leahy |date=June 10, 2007 |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/06/05/AR2007060501702_pf.html}}</ref>

=== Election to Congress ===

Courtney [[United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2006#District 2|challenged Simmons again in 2006]], in a race that political strategists projected would be very close. Courtney was declared the winner on Election Day; initial tallies showed him ahead by 167 votes, out of more than 242,000 votes. Under Connecticut law the race qualified for an automatic re-canvass because the winning margin was less than one half percent. The [[recount]], finished on November 14, showed Courtney with 91 votes more than Simmons.<ref>{{cite news |author=Jennifer Medina|title= Democrat Wins House Seat After Recount in Connecticut |newspaper= New York Times |date=November 16, 2006 | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/11/16/nyregion/16recount.html}}</ref>

=== Re-elections ===

==== 2008 ====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2008#District 2}}

Courtney's 2008 [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] challenger was the former [[Captain (United States)|commanding officer]] of the [[Naval Submarine Base New London]], [[Sean Sullivan (naval officer)|Sean Sullivan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cpbn.org/program/where-we-live/episode/sean-sullivan|title=2nd District Challenger Sean Sullivan|last=Dankosky|first=John|author2=Blanksteen, Melissa|date=2008-04-15|work=Where We Live|publisher=Connecticut Public Broadcasting|accessdate=2008-10-13}}</ref> Courtney won the election by a two-to-one margin.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theday.com/re.aspx?re=81c2a8e2-250a-4130-946f-7dcfa5c9fba7|title=This Time, Courtney Cruises|last=Mann|first=Ted|date=2008-11-05|work=[[The New London Day]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.norwichbulletin.com/news/x635427302/Decision-2008-No-doubt-this-time-Courtney-wins-big|title=Decision 2008: No doubt this time — Courtney wins big|last=Gannon|first=Michael|date=2008-11-04|work=[[Norwich Bulletin]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.courant.com/news/politics/hc-2house.artnov05,0,1669661.story|title=Courtney Re-Elected To Congress By Wide Margin|last=Mahony|first=Edmund H.|coauthors=Funkhouser ,David and Marteka, Peter |date=2008-11-05|work=[[The Hartford Courant]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref>

On May 21, 2008, he announced his endorsement of then-[[U.S. Senator]] [[Barack Obama]] for president.

==== 2010 ====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2010#District 2}}

Courtney was challenged by Republican nominee [[Janet Peckinpaugh]], Green Party nominee Scott Deshefy, and Libertarian write-in Dan Reale. Courtney was re-elected to another term.<ref>[http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/files/elections/2010/general/by_county/us_house/CT.html?SITE=CTHARELN&SECTION=POLITICS AP Election Results - Courant.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

====2014====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2014#District 2}}

Courtney will face off against businesswoman [[Lori Hopkins-Cavanagh]] in November. Hopkins-Cavanagh is a business owner and radio show host from [[New London, Connecticut]]. 

==U.S. House of Representatives==

=== Political issues ===
In 2010, Courtney was the leading voice in the House against the so-called "Cadillac tax" on high-dollar health plans, part of the funding proposed for the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].{{cite web|title='Cadillac' Tax' Puts Obama on Collision Course With House Democrats|url=http://www.politicsdaily.com/2010/01/11/cadillac-tax-puts-obama-on-collision-course-with-house-democr/|publisher=Politics Daily|author=Patricia Murphy|date=January 11, 2010}}

===Committee assignments===
*'''[[United States House Committee on Agriculture|Committee on Agriculture]]'''
**[[United States House Agriculture Subcommittee on General Farm Commodities and Risk Management|Subcommittee on General Farm Commodities and Risk Management]]
**[[United States House Agriculture Subcommittee on Livestock, Dairy, and Poultry|Subcommittee on Livestock, Dairy, and Poultry]]
*'''[[United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]]'''
**[[United States House Armed Services Subcommittee on Readiness|Subcommittee on Readiness]]
**[[United States House Armed Services Subcommittee on Seapower and Expeditionary Forces|Subcommittee on Seapower and Expeditionary Forces]]

Prior to the 112th Congress, Courtney served on the [[United States House Committee on Education and Labor|Committee on Education and Labor]] with membership on the [[United States House Education Subcommittee on Higher Education, Lifelong Learning, and Competitiveness|Subcommittee on Higher Education, Lifelong Learning, and Competitiveness]] and [[United States House Education Subcommittee on Health, Employment, Labor, and Pensions|Subcommittee on Health, Employment, Labor, and Pensions]].

==Personal ==
Courtney lives with his wife, a [[nurse practitioner]], and two children in [[Vernon, Connecticut|Vernon]], a suburb of [[Hartford]].

'''Joseph "Joe" Courtney''' (born April 6, 1953) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|Connecticut|2}}, serving since 2007.  The district includes most of the eastern third of the state, including [[Norwich, Connecticut|Norwich]] and [[New London, Connecticut|New London]].  He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].

==Early life and education ==

Courtney grew up in suburban Hartford. In 1975 he graduated from [[Tufts University]]. He earned a law degree from the [[University of Connecticut School of Law]] in 1978.

== Career ==

=== Prior to Congress ===
After graduating from law school, Courtney worked as a public defender for three years. He became a partner in the law firm Courtney, Boyan & Foran, and also served as Town Attorney in [[Vernon, Connecticut]], where he lives.

From 1987 to 1994, Courtney served in the [[Connecticut House of Representatives]], representing Connecticut's 56th district. He was chairman of the Public Health and Human Service Committee and oversaw the Blue Ribbon Commission on Universal Health Insurance. In 1994, he was honored by [[Connecticut Magazine]] for his bipartisan efforts in the state house.

In 1998, after four years out of office, Courtney made an unsuccessful bid for [[Lieutenant Governor of Connecticut|Lieutenant Governor]]. In 2002, he ran for Congress against incumbent Republican [[Rob Simmons]]. Courtney did not announce his candidacy or raise money until September 2001; by that time, Simmons had more than $500,000 in campaign funding. Simmons defeated Courtney in the November election, 54% to 46%.<ref>{{cite news |title=House Rules|newspaper=Washington Post |author= Michael Leahy |date=June 10, 2007 |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/06/05/AR2007060501702_pf.html}}</ref>

=== Election to Congress ===

Courtney [[United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2006#District 2|challenged Simmons again in 2006]], in a race that political strategists projected would be very close. Courtney was declared the winner on Election Day; initial tallies showed him ahead by 167 votes, out of more than 242,000 votes. Under Connecticut law the race qualified for an automatic re-canvass because the winning margin was less than one half percent. The [[recount]], finished on November 14, showed Courtney with 91 votes more than Simmons.<ref>{{cite news |author=Jennifer Medina|title= Democrat Wins House Seat After Recount in Connecticut |newspaper= New York Times |date=November 16, 2006 | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/11/16/nyregion/16recount.html}}</ref>

=== Re-elections ===

==== 2008 ====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2008#District 2}}

Courtney's 2008 [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] challenger was the former [[Captain (United States)|commanding officer]] of the [[Naval Submarine Base New London]], [[Sean Sullivan (naval officer)|Sean Sullivan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cpbn.org/program/where-we-live/episode/sean-sullivan|title=2nd District Challenger Sean Sullivan|last=Dankosky|first=John|author2=Blanksteen, Melissa|date=2008-04-15|work=Where We Live|publisher=Connecticut Public Broadcasting|accessdate=2008-10-13}}</ref> Courtney won the election by a two-to-one margin.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theday.com/re.aspx?re=81c2a8e2-250a-4130-946f-7dcfa5c9fba7|title=This Time, Courtney Cruises|last=Mann|first=Ted|date=2008-11-05|work=[[The New London Day]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.norwichbulletin.com/news/x635427302/Decision-2008-No-doubt-this-time-Courtney-wins-big|title=Decision 2008: No doubt this time — Courtney wins big|last=Gannon|first=Michael|date=2008-11-04|work=[[Norwich Bulletin]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.courant.com/news/politics/hc-2house.artnov05,0,1669661.story|title=Courtney Re-Elected To Congress By Wide Margin|last=Mahony|first=Edmund H.|coauthors=Funkhouser ,David and Marteka, Peter |date=2008-11-05|work=[[The Hartford Courant]]|accessdate=2008-11-05}}</ref>

On May 21, 2008, he announced his endorsement of then-[[U.S. Senator]] [[Barack Obama]] for president.

==== 2010 ====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2010#District 2}}

Courtney was challenged by Republican nominee [[Janet Peckinpaugh]], Green Party nominee Scott Deshefy, and Libertarian write-in Dan Reale. Courtney was re-elected to another term.<ref>[http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/files/elections/2010/general/by_county/us_house/CT.html?SITE=CTHARELN&SECTION=POLITICS AP Election Results - Courant.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

====2014====

{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Connecticut, 2014#District 2}}

Courtney will face off against businesswoman [[Lori Hopkins-Cavanagh]] in November. Hopkins-Cavanagh is a business owner and radio show host from [[New London, Connecticut]]. 

==U.S. House of Representatives==

=== Political issues ===
In 2010, Courtney was the leading voice in the House against the so-called "Cadillac tax" on high-dollar health plans, part of the funding proposed for the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].{{cite web|title='Cadillac' Tax' Puts Obama on Collision Course With House Democrats|url=http://www.politicsdaily.com/2010/01/11/cadillac-tax-puts-obama-on-collision-course-with-house-democr/|publisher=Politics Daily|author=Patricia Murphy|date=January 11, 2010}}

Courtney lives with his wife, a [[nurse practitioner]], and two children in [[Vernon, Connecticut|Vernon]], a suburb of [[Hartford]].
