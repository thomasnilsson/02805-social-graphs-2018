'''Allen Lucas "Luke" Messer''' (born February 27, 1969) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has served as a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]], representing [[Indiana's 6th congressional district]], since 2013. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].

Born in [[Evansville, Indiana]], Messer is a graduate from [[Wabash College]] and [[Vanderbilt University Law School]]. After a failed run for the U.S. House in 2000, Messer served as the executive director of the [[Indiana Republican Party]] from 2001 to 2005. He was appointed to serve in the [[Indiana House of Representatives]] in 2003, after State Representative [[W. Roland Stine]] was killed in a car accident. Messer represented Indiana's 57th District from 2003 to 2007. In 2012, Messer defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] challenger Brad Bookout and was elected to a seat in the U.S. House of Representatives.

==Early life, education, and law career==
Messer graduated from Greensburg Community High School in 1987.<ref name="in.gov">{{cite web | url=http://www.in.gov/legislative/house_republicans/newsroom/luke.html | title =Luke Messer Sworn in Today  as State Representative for House District 57 | publisher =in.gov | date =May 28, 2003 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref>

Messer attended [[Wabash College]] where he was a member of the [[Phi Delta Theta]] Fraternity and majored in speech, he graduated in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Torrez|first1=Jonathan|title=The Wabash College Bachelor|url=http://www.wabash.edu/bachelor/issues/2006f/fullpaper.pdf|website=wabash.edu|publisher=Wabash College Board of Publications|accessdate=9 December 2014}}</ref> He also attended  [[Vanderbilt University Law School]], from which he received a law degree, in 1994.<ref name="nationaljournal.com">{{cite web|url= http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/indiana-6th-house-district-20121101
|title=Indiana’s 6th House District Luke Messer bio; The National Journal |publisher=nationaljournal.com |date= |accessdate=2012-12-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/indystar/access/1913977181.html?FMT=CITE&FMTS=CITE:FT&type=current&date=Oct+14%2C+1999&author=&pub=Indianapolis+Star&desc=Ex-McIntosh+aide+is+seeking+GOP+nod+to+fill+seat&pqatl=google |title=Ex-McIntosh aide is seeking GOP nod to fill seat |publisher=Pqasb.pqarchiver.com |date=1999-10-14 |accessdate=2012-09-15}}</ref> Shortly after Messer graduated from [[Vanderbilt University|Vanderbilt]], he served as an Associate Counsel at [[Koch Industries]] from 1995 to 1996.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.solitical.com/official/details/33997 | title =LUKE MESSER INFORMATION | publisher =solitical.com | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref>

==Early political career==
Messer started his political career serving as the Press Secretary for Tennessee Representative [[Ed Bryant]] in 1997. He served as the Legal Counsel on the [[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|House Subcommittee for Government Reform]] for Indiana Representative's [[David M. McIntosh|David McIntosh]] and [[Dan Burton]] from 1998 to 1999, and the Legal Counsel to U.S. Representative [[Jimmy Duncan (U.S. politician)|Jim Duncan]] later in 1999.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/33997/luke-messer#.UPjqSyfol5I | title =REPRESENTATIVE LUKE MESSER'S BIOGRAPHY | publisher =votesmart.org | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> In 1998, he served as the campaign manager for [[Virginia Murphy Blankenbaker]]'s failed congressional campaign.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.gwu.edu/~action/2004/bush/bushorgin.html | title =President George W. Bush-Campaign Organization, Indiana | publisher =georgewashingtonuniversity.edu | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> In 1999, Messer returned to Indiana and was a practicing attorney at the Barnes & Thornburg Law Firm in [[Indianapolis]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.wabash.edu/alumni/news.cfm?news_ID=1142 | title =Wabash Alumnus Elected to Indiana General Assembly | publisher =wabash.edu | date =May 22, 2003 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref>

In 2000, Messer ran for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in [[Indiana's 2nd congressional district]], where [[incumbent]] David M. McIntosh was retiring to [[Indiana gubernatorial election, 2000|run for governor]]. Though Messer received the endorsement of ''[[The Indianapolis Star]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/indystar/access/1912924381.html?FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+17%2C+2000&author=&pub=Indianapolis+Star&desc=Candidate+Messer's+good+ideas+for+positive+change&pqatl=google |title=Candidate Messer's good ideas for positive change |publisher=Pqasb.pqarchiver.com |date=2000-04-17 |accessdate=2012-09-15}}</ref> he lost the election to [[Mike Pence]].

==Indiana House of Representatives==
On May 23, 2003, Messer was sworn in by the Chief Justice of [[Indiana Supreme Court]] [[Randall Shepard]] to fill the remainder of the late [[W. Roland Stine]]'s term.<ref name="in.gov"/> From 2003-2006, Messer represented District 57 in the [[Indiana House of Representatives]], which contained portions of [[Shelby County, Indiana|Shelby County]] and [[Bartholomew County]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=IBNP&p_multi=IBJB&p_theme=ibnp&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=11206C3BC1F461A8&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |title=IBJ Newsbank Archives |publisher=Nl.newsbank.com |date=2005-02-07 |accessdate=2012-09-14}}</ref> During the 2005-2006 legislative session, Messer served as the Assistant Majority Floor Leader.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.state.in.us/legislative/house_republicans/newsroom/commchairs.html | title =INDIANA HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES COMMITTEE CHAIRS // 2005-2006 | publisher =state.in.us | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> His legislation aimed at curbing high school drop-out rates received national attention after Shelbyville High School became a symbol of a national dropout crisis.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1181646,00.html
|title=Drop Out Nation; Time Magazine |publisher=www.time.com |date=2006-04-09 |accessdate=2012-12-09}}</ref> He did not run for reelection as State Representative in 2006, and was succeeded by [[Sean Eberhart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/indystar/access/1754670871.html?FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Oct+31%2C+2006&author=&pub=Indianapolis+Star&desc=Election+2006%3A+Indiana+House&pqatl=google |title=Election 2006: Indiana House |publisher=Pqasb.pqarchiver.com |date=2006-10-31 |accessdate=2012-09-15}}</ref> Messer joined the law firm [[Ice Miller]] LLP as a partner in their Public Affairs group.<ref>{{cite web|author=InsideIndianaBusiness.com Report |url=http://www.insideindianabusiness.com/newsitem.asp?ID=17596 |title=State Representative Luke Messer Joins Ice Miller - Newsroom - Inside INdiana Business with Gerry Dick |publisher=Insideindianabusiness.com |date= |accessdate=2012-09-15}}</ref>

Messer served as the co-chair of [[John McCain]]'s [[John McCain presidential campaign, 2008|2008 presidential campaign in Indiana]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Davies |first=Tom |url=http://seattletimes.com/html/politics/2008199303_apflippingindiana.html |title=Politics &#124; Obama out to flip Indiana to Dems &#124; Seattle Times Newspaper |publisher=Seattletimes.com |date=2008-09-24 |accessdate=2012-09-15}}</ref> In 2010, Messer ran for the House of Representatives  in [[Indiana's 5th congressional district]]. He challenged [[Dan Burton]], the incumbent representative, in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Indiana, 2010#District 5|Republican primary]]. Burton narrowly defeated Messer in the election.<ref name=is2>{{cite web|url=http://www.indystar.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=2011107090316|title=Candidates line up for Pence's open seat in Congress|date=July 9, 2011|accessdate=July 11, 2011|work=[[The Indianapolis Star]]|first=Mary Beth|last=Schneider}}</ref> Messer then became President & CEO of School Choice Indiana, a lobbying group that supported Indiana's private school voucher law.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Messer-defeats-Bookout-wins-Pence-s-6th-seat-4014932.php|title=Messer defeats Bookout, wins Pence's 6th seat|agency=Associated Press|newspaper=San Francisco Chronicle|date=November 6, 2012|accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref>

==United States House of Representatives==

===Elections===
With the announcement of then Representative [[Mike Pence]]'s intentions to run for [[Governor of Indiana]], in May 2011, Messer declared his candidacy for the Republican nomination to represent the 6th District.  His home in Shelbyville had been shifted from the 5th District to the 6th District in redistricting.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.latimes.com/topic/wxin-luke-messer-former-state-lawmaker-announces-run-for-congress-20110524,0,1928635.story?track=rss-topicgallery | title =Former state lawmaker announces run for Congress | publisher =''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' | date =May 21, 2011 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> On May 8, 2012 Messer defeated the grassroots effort by [[Columbus, Indiana|Columbus]] real estate investor Travis Hankins, winning with 71% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.indystar.com/article/20120508/NEWS0502/120508046 | title =Messer conquers crowded Republican field in 6th District | publisher =indystar.com | date =May 8, 2012 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> He faced [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] Brad Bookout, a [[Delaware County, Indiana|Delaware County]] councilman, in the general election.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestarpress.com/article/20120509/ELECTIONS/120509003/Bookout-face-young-gun-Messer-race-U-S-Congress-seat |title=Bookout to face 'young gun' Messer in race for U.S. Congress seat &#124; The Star Press |publisher=thestarpress.com |date= |accessdate=2012-06-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestarpress.com/article/20120508/ELECTIONS/120508018/Frazier-losing-Bookout-winning-Delaware-County-6th-District |title=Messer, Bookout win vote in Delaware County for U.S. Congress seat &#124; The Star Press |publisher=thestarpress.com |date= |accessdate=2012-06-03}}</ref> Messer, described as a "Libertarian-Leaning Conservative" by [[OnTheIssues|OnTheIssues.Org]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ontheissues.org/IN/Luke_Messer.htm | title =IN Luke Messer | publisher =ontheissues.org | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> aligned his views with the views of Mike Pence, saying that "If you like Congressman Mike Pence, we’re going to have very similar philosophy in the way we approach the job," he was also described by Bookout as "a clone" of Pence.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://indianapublicmedia.org/news/6th-district-candidates-compare-mike-pence-38942/ | title =6th District Candidates Compare Themselves To Mike Pence | publisher =indianapublicmedia.org | date =October 29, 2012 | accessdate =January 17, 2013}}</ref> On November 6, 2012, Messer defeated Bookout with roughly 59% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.therepublic.com/view/local_story/Messer-puts-focus-on-jobs-budg_1352270428|title=Messer puts focus on jobs, budget work|last=Johannesen|first=Kirk|date=November 7, 2012|work=The Republic|location=Columbus, Indiana}}</ref> After the election, Messer was selected by his peers as Freshman Class President for the 113th Congress.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indystar.com/article/20121115/NEWS05/121115026/Indiana-s-new-federal-lawmakers-seek-leadership-roles-learn-ropes |title=Indiana’s-new-federal-lawmakers-seek-leadership-roles-learn-ropes; The Indianapolis Star |publisher=indystar.com |date= |accessdate=2012-11-15}}</ref>

===Tenure===
In 2013 Messer signed a pledge sponsored by [[Americans for Prosperity]] promising to vote against any Global Warming legislation that would raise taxes.<ref>http://americansforprosperity.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Messer_Luke.pdf</ref>

==Personal life==
Messer is married to his wife Jennifer, have two daughters and one son, and they currently reside in [[Shelbyville, Indiana]]. Messer is the author of a children’s book called ''Hoosier Heart''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/luke-messer--IN-H
