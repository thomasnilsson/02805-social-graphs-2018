'''Joseph John “Joe” Heck''' (born October 30, 1961) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Nevada's 3rd congressional district]] since 2011. Heck, a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], is a board-certified [[physician]] and served as a [[Nevada Senate|Nevada State Senator]] from 2004 to 2008.

Heck's congressional district includes the cities of [[Henderson, Nevada|Henderson]], [[Boulder City]], parts of [[Las Vegas metropolitan area|Las Vegas]], unincorporated [[Clark County, Nevada|Clark County]], and several smaller towns and cities in the Southern Nevada region. He resides in Henderson, Nevada.

==Early life, education, and military service==
Heck was born in [[Jamaica, New York]] in [[Queens, New York|Queens]] and raised in Pennsylvania. In 1992, he moved to Clark County, Nevada. He graduated from the [[Pennsylvania State University]] in 1984 with a degree in [[Health Education]]. He is a member of the [[Beta Theta Pi]] fraternity. He received his [[Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine]] in 1988 from the [[Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine]] and obtained a residency in [[Emergency Medicine]] in 1992 at the [[Albert Einstein Medical Center]]. He earned a [[Strategic Studies|Masters of Strategic Studies]] degree from the [[U.S. Army War College]] in 2006.<ref>[http://www.heck4nevada.com/bio Heck campaign website]</ref>

As of July 31st, 2014, Heck is a brigadier general in the [[United States Army Reserve]]. He has commanded a Medical Readiness Support Group overseeing more than 2,000 soldiers in 6 western states, and continued to serve in this capacity while in Congress. He has served in [[Operation Joint Endeavor]], [[Operation Noble Eagle]], and [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]]. His last deployment was January, 2008.<ref name="Official Biography">[http://heck.house.gov/about-me/full-biography Official Biography]</ref>

==Medical career==
Heck was the president, owner, and medical director of Specialized Medical Operations until 2011. The company provided medical training, consulting, and operational support to law enforcement agencies, EMS, and military special operations. Heck has lectured and is published on special operations medical support, the medical response to acts of terrorism, and emergency preparedness and response.<ref name="Official Biography"/>

From 1998 to 2003, Heck served as the medical director of the Casualty Care Research Center of the [[Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences]] in Bethesda, Maryland, where he provided medical support for several federal law enforcement agencies and oversight for the medical response to acts of terrorism.

Heck started his medical career as a [[volunteer firefighter]] and ambulance attendant in rural Pennsylvania. He volunteered as a Medical Team Manager with the Nevada Urban Search & Rescue Team&nbsp;— Task Force 1 and as a member of the [[Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department]] Search & Rescue team. He has also served as a tactical physician with the LVMPD SWAT team.

Heck served as a member of the Nevada State Homeland Security Commission Sub-committee on Health, the [[American Osteopathic Association|American Osteopathic Association's]] Task Force on Bioterrorism, and as the Medical Director for the Nevada Hospital Association’s Hospital Preparedness program. He also served as the Medical Director for the Southern Nevada Health District's Office of Public Health Preparedness.<ref name="leg.state.nv.us">[http://www.leg.state.nv.us/73rd/Legislators/Senators/Heck.cfm Nevada State Senate Legislative Biography]</ref>

==Nevada Senate==
Heck served one four-year term in the Nevada Senate, representing Clark County’s 5th district.<ref name="leg.state.nv.us"/>

===Elections===
Heck was first elected to the Nevada Senate to represent Clark County’s 5th district in 2004, after defeating Senator Ann O’Connell in the Republican primary. Heck narrowly lost reelection in 2008 to [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Shirley Breeden]] by a margin of 46.57% to 45.81% and a plurality of 765 votes. Libertarian T. Rex Hagan received 4754 votes (8.4%).<ref>[http://sos.state.nv.us/elections/results/2008StateWideGeneral/ElectionSummary.asp/ Nevada Senate 2008 Election Results] Nevada Secretary of State.</ref>

===Committee assignments===
He served on the Natural Resources, Human Resources and Education, the Commerce and Labor Committees. He was also the vice-chair of the Transportation and Homeland Security Committee.

==U.S. House of Representatives==

===Elections===
;2010
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2010#District 3|Nevada gubernatorial election, 2010}}
Although Heck had earlier announced he would challenge incumbent Republican [[Jim Gibbons (U. S. politician)|Jim Gibbons]] for Governor, he decided against it in favor of a run for [[Nevada's 3rd congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kvbc.com/Global/story.asp?S=11272285|title=Home&nbsp;— My News 3 - KSNV, Las Vegas, Nevada|publisher=Kvbc.com|date=|accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref>  He defeated incumbent Democratic U.S. Congresswoman [[Dina Titus]], 48%-47%, a difference of 1,748 votes. Titus had only held the position for one term after she defeated incumbent Republican U.S. Congressman [[Jon C. Porter]] in 2008.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=496881</ref>

;2012
{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2012#District 3}}
After redistricting, Heck decided to run in the newly redrawn 3rd district, which Obama won in 2008 with 54% of the vote. On November 6, he defeated [[Speaker of the Nevada House of Representatives|Speaker of the state Assembly]] [[John Oceguera]] 50%-43%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705010</ref>

===Tenure===
Heck was one of three freshmen named to the House Republican Steering Committee in the 112th Congress.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegassun.com/blogs/nevada-wonk/2010/nov/17/joe-heck-lands-plum-post-house-gop-steering-commit/|title=Joe Heck Lands Plum Post on House GOP Steering Committee|publisher=LasVegasSun.com|date=|accessdate=2011-01-24}}</ref>

;Economic issues
Heck has indicated that he has a plan to fast track tourist visas to boost the tourist industry in Southern Nevada. He has noted his opposition to government tax burdens and red tape on on the private sector.<ref name="heck4nevada.com">Heck on the Issues.” Heck4Nevada.com, n.d. http://heck4nevada.com/issues</ref> Heck supports an audit of the Federal Reserve and a [[Balanced budget amendment|balanced budget amendment to the Constitution]].<ref name="heck4nevada.com"/> He was among only 10 House Republicans who opposed Budget Committee Chairman [[Paul Ryan]]’s 2012 plan. Heck also supports Nevada Governor [[Brian Sandoval]]’s decision to accept federal money to expand Medicaid coverage.

In 2011 Heck voted in favor of Free Trade Agreements and Trade Promotion Agreements with [[United States-Korea Free Trade Agreement|Korea]], [[United States-Colombia Free Trade Agreement|Colombia]], and [[United States-Panama relations|Panama]]. Heck voted against increasing the debt limit and supported a separate bill that expressed disapproval of the President’s authority to raise the debt limit.<ref>{{cite web|title=Heck Votes Against Debt Limit Increase|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/612747/heck-votes-against-debt-limit-increase|accessdate=21 November 2011}}</ref>

;Health care
Heck opposes [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]], which he has voted to repeal.<ref>{{cite web|title=Joe Heck, Jr.'s Issue Positions|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/political-courage-test/44082/joe-heck-jr/#.U1bgvcdNpeE|work=Projet Vote Smart|accessdate=22 April 2014}}</ref>  His priorities include protecting the patient-physician relationship, reducing health care costs, and working to protect Medicare for Nevada’s seniors and preserving it for future generations.<ref name="heck4nevada.com"/>

;Energy
Heck voted to prohibit the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] from regulating greenhouse gasses.<ref>{{cite web|title=House Vote 249 - Prevents E.P.A. From Regulating Greenhouse Gases|url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/112/house/1/249?ref=politics|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=22 April 2014}}</ref> He has signed [[Americans for Prosperity]]'s No Climate Tax pledge.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pledge Takers|url=http://www.noclimatetax.com/pledge-signatories/|publisher=Americans for Prosperity|accessdate=22 April 2014}}</ref>   Heck attributes increased energy costs to environmental regulation.<ref>{{cite web|title=Issue Position: Energy|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/626589/issue-position-energy|accessdate=21 November 2011}}</ref>

;Foreign policy
In 2011, Heck voted for the [[National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2012]].<ref>http://www.ibtimes.com/ndaa-bill-how-did-your-congress-member-vote-384362</ref>

Heck was opposed to United States involvement in Libya, saying, “We are already engaged in military operations on two fronts, and Libya opened a third. We cannot afford the troops or taxpayer dollars—especially without a national security objective.” <ref>{{cite web|title=Heck Votes Against Libya Measures|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/622143/heck-votes-against-libya-measures|accessdate=21 November 2011}}</ref>

;Social policy
In 2011, Heck voted in favor of amending the federal health care law to prohibit abortion coverage, prohibiting taxpayer funding of abortion, and prohibiting the use of federal funds for Planned Parenthood. He supported the [[Violence Against Women Act]].<ref>{{cite news | author=Jennifer Bendery | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/12/11/violence-against-women-act-john-boehner-eric-cantor_n_2278549.html | title=Violence Against Women Act: John Boehner, Eric Cantor Pressured By Republicans To Act | date=11 December 2012 | publisher=Huffington Post}}</ref>

;Immigration
In August 2014, Heck broke ranks with the Republican Party and voted against a bill that would have dismantled the [[Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Foley|first1=Elise|title=House Votes To Strip Deportation Relief From Dreamers|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/01/house-bill-daca_n_5643287.html|accessdate=4 September 2014|publisher=Huffington Post|date=8-1-2014}}</ref>

===Sponsored legislation===
The following is a partial list of legislation that was directly sponsored by Heck.
*[[Three Kids Mine Remediation and Reclamation Act (H.R. 697; 113th Congress)]] - a bill that would authorize the sale of approximately 950 acres of federal land to the city of [[Henderson, Nevada]].<ref name=697sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 697 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/697|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=22 July 2013}}</ref>  The land used to be a mine and now needs significant environmental remediation and reclamation.<ref name=cbo697>{{cite web|title=CBO&nbsp;— H.R. 697|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/44357|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=22 July 2013}}</ref>
*[[Vulnerable Veterans Housing Reform Act of 2013 (H.R. 1742; 113th Congress)]] - a bill that would change the way the [[United States Department of Housing and Urban Development]] (HUD) calculates a veterans' income to exclude some aid that some veterans receive from the [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs]] (VA).<ref name=nlihcMay3>{{cite web|title=Bills to Address Housing Needs of Veterans Introduced|url=http://nlihc.org/article/bills-address-housing-needs-veterans-introduced|publisher=National Low Income Housing Coalition|accessdate=28 October 2013|date=3 May 2013}}</ref> The bill would also change utility allowances.<ref name=1742sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 1742|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/1742|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=28 October 2013}}</ref>
*[[Good Samaritan Search and Recovery Act of 2013 (H.R. 2166; 113th Congress)]] - a bill that would require the federal government to issue the appropriate permits within 48 hours to volunteer search and rescue groups to allow them to search [[federal lands]] for [[missing person]]s.<ref name=2166sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 2166 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th/house-bill/2166|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=27 January 2014}}</ref><ref name=houselifthurdles>{{cite news|last=Kasperowicz|first=Pete|title=House to lift government hurdles to missing person searches|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/196357-house-to-lift-government-hurdles-to-missing-person-searches|accessdate=27 January 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=24 January 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Joe and his wife, Lisa (formerly Mattiello), have three children: Monica, Chelsea and Joseph III. Lisa is a registered nurse. Heck is a [[Roman Catholic]]. He is active with the [[Knights of Columbus]] Council 13456 and Catholic War Veterans Post 1947.<ref name="Official Biography"/>
