A former [[Federal Bureau of Investigation|FBI]] agent, he was elected in [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2016|2016]] and took office on January 3, 2017.\n\n==Early life and education==\nBorn in [[Philadelphia]] and raised in nearby [[Levittown, Pennsylvania]], Fitzpatrick graduated from [[Bishop Egan High School]] in [[Fairless Hills, Pennsylvania|Fairless Hills]] in 1992.<ref name=bioguide>{{cite web|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=F000466|title=Fitzpatrick, Brian K. (1973- )|work=Biographical Directory of the United States Congress|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=July 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name=aims>{{cite web|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/news/politics/20160122_Fitzpatrick_s_brother_aims_to_succeed_him_in_U_S__House.html|title=Fitzpatrick's brother aims to succeed him in U.S. House|last=Tamari|first=Jonathan|work=Philadelphia Inquirer|date=January 22, 2016|accessdate=July 22, 2018}}</ref> He graduated from [[La Salle University]] in 1996 with a [[Bachelor of Science in Business Administration]]. In 2001, Fitzpatrick completed both a [[Master of Business Administration]] at [[Pennsylvania State University]] and a [[Juris Doctor]] at the [[Pennsylvania State University - Dickinson Law|Penn State Dickinson School of Law]].<ref name=\"buckslocalnews\">{{cite web|url=http://www.buckslocalnews.com/articles/2016/01/24/bucks_news/doc56a16ab9a0d5e721510104.txt|title=Brian Fitzpatrick announces candidacy for Congress in Pennsylvania's 8th District|website=BucksLocalNews.com|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref><ref name=bioguide />\n\n==Career==\nFitzpatrick is a former Special Assistant [[United States Attorney]] and [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]] (FBI) supervisory special agent in California. At the FBI, he served as a national supervisor for the Bureau's Public Corruption Unit, and led the agency's Campaign Finance and Election Crimes Enforcement program. During his time in the FBI, he spent time in [[Kiev]], [[Ukraine]], [[Mosul]], [[Iraq]] and Washington, D.C.<ref name=\"buckslocalnews\"/> He was embedded with U.S. Special Forces as part of [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]].\n\n==House of Representatives==\n===Elections===\n====2016====\nIn 2016, Fitzpatrick ran for the open U.S. House of Representatives seat of his brother [[Mike Fitzpatrick]], who retired from Congress to uphold a promise to limit himself to four terms.<ref name=\"politicspa\">{{cite web|url=http://www.politicspa.com/pa-8-report-fitzpatricks-brother-to-seek-seat/72119|title=PA-8: Report: Fitzpatrick's Brother to Seek Seat|website=PoliticsPA.com|date=2016-01-21|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref><ref name=\"phillycom\">{{cite web|last=Tamari|first=Jonathan|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/blogs/capitolinq/Is-Rep-Fitzpatricks-brother-running-to-replace-him.html|title=Rep. Fitzpatrick's brother will run to replace him|website=Philly.com|date=2016-01-21|accessdate=2016-11-11}}</ref>\n\nIn the April 26, 2016, Republican primary, Fitzpatrick received 78.4% of the vote, defeating Andy Warren and Marc Duome. Meanwhile, Steve Santarsiero defeated Shaughnessy Naughton for the Democratic nomination, 59.8% to 40.2%. In the general election, Fitzpatrick received 54.4% of the vote, while Santarsiero received 45.6%.<ref>https://ballotpedia.org/Pennsylvania%27s_8th_Congressional_District_election,_2016</ref>\n\n====2018====\nAs a result of redistricting, Fitzpatrick is running for Congress in [[Pennsylvania's 1st congressional district]] in 2018. In the Republican primary on May 15, 2018, he defeated Dean Malik by a margin of 68.85% to 31.15%. In the Democratic primary, [[Scott Wallace (politician)|Scott Wallace]] won with 55.97% of the vote, defeating Rachel Reddick, who tallied 35.85%, and Steve Bacher, who brought in 8.18%.<ref>https://ballotpedia.org/Brian_Fitzpatrick</ref> Fitzpatrick and Wallace are now running against each other in the [[United States elections, 2018|2018 midterm election]].\n\n===Political positions===\nIn the first session of the [[115th United States Congress]], Fitzpatrick was ranked the third most bipartisan member of the [[United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] by the Bipartisan Index, a metric created by the Lugar Center and [[Georgetown University|Georgetown's]] [[McCourt School of Public Policy]] to assess [[United States Congress|congressional]] bipartisanship.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thelugarcenter.org/assets/htmldocuments/House%20Scores%20115th%20Congress%20First%20Session.pdf|publisher=The Lugar Center|title=The Lugar Center - McCourt School Bipartisan Index|date=April 24, 2018|access-date=July 2, 2018|location=Washington, D.C.}}</ref> GovTrack noted that Fitzpatrick introduced the most bills among freshman Representatives, and, of the 274 bills he cosponsored, 35% were introduced by a non-Republican legislator.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/brian_fitzpatrick/412721/report-card/2017|title=Rep. Brian Fitzpatrick\u2019s 2017 Report Card|publisher=GovTrack|location=Washington, D.C.|date=January 6, 2018|accessdate=July 2, 2018}}</ref>\n\n====Tax Reform====\nIn December 2017, Fitzpatrick voted in favor of the Republicans\u2019 tax overhaul bill in a party-line vote.<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html</ref> It was the most sweeping tax overhaul in 31 years, and the tax bill delivers deep and permanent tax cuts for corporations while providing temporary tax cuts for individual taxpayers, including the wealthiest Americans.<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/2017/12/20/us/politics/tax-bill-republicans.html</ref>\n\n====Climate change====\nAt a September 2018 forum hosted by the [[Bipartisan Policy Center]] and ''[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]'', Fitzpatrick highlighted man made [[climate change]] as a serious issue. He stated that Republicans must \"acknowledge reality and don't deny it.\" He is a member of the bipartisan congressional [[Climate Solutions Caucus]] and has crafted legislation to implement a carbon tax in the U.S.<ref>{{cite web|title=90 Current Climate Solutions Caucus Members|author=|url=https://citizensclimatelobby.org/climate-solutions-caucus/|format=| publisher=Citizen's Climate Lobby |date=|accessdate=20 October 2018}}</ref><ref>https://eos.org/articles/republican-congressman-urges-colleagues-to-act-on-climate-change</ref>\n\n====Immigration====\nIn 2017, Fitzpatrick was critical of President Obama's executive order establishing the DACA program, but said the immigration system was broken. In a 2018 debate, Fitzpatrick said that he supported a path to citizenship for DREAMers, but that \"any immigration reform package has to deal with border security.\"<ref name=\"whyy.org\">Benshoff, Laura; Bucks Republicans clash over Russia, immigration in congressional debate; Whyy; May 2, 2018; https://whyy.org/articles/bucks-republicans-clash-over-russia-immigration-in-congressional-debate/</ref><ref>Boyle, james; Rep. Brian Fitzpatrick, Pennsylvania lawmakers call for legislative DACA solution; The Intelligence; September 5, 2017; http://www.theintell.com/a84a0884-924b-11e7-8edd-d3f86181ed08.html</ref>\n\nFitzpatrick opposed President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to impose a temporary ban on entry to the U.S. to citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries. He stated that \"the president's policy entirely misses the mark.\"<ref name=\"Timmons\">{{cite web|last1=Timmons|first1=Heather|title=The short (but growing) list of Republican lawmakers who are publicly condemning Trump's \"Muslim ban\"|website=Quartz|accessdate=31 January 2017}}</ref>\n\n====Russia====\nIn a 2018 debate, Fitzpatrick said that Russia held \"by and large, sinister motives\", noting that while he was stationed in Ukraine, twice Russia attempted to knock out the nation's electrical grids through cyber attacks.<ref name=\"whyy.org\"/>\n\nIn July 2018, Fitzpatrick said that President Trump had been \"manipulated\" by Russian leader [[Vladimir Putin]] at the Helsinki Summit. Fitzpatrick said he was \"frankly sickened by the exchange\" between Trump and Putin. He criticized the \"mixed signals\" that the Trump administration was sending regarding Russian interference in the 2016 election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/22/631326586/gop-rep-brian-fitzpatrick-the-president-was-manipulated-by-vladimir-putin|title=GOP Rep. Brian Fitzpatrick: 'The President Was Manipulated By Vladimir Putin'|work=NPR.org|access-date=2018-07-24|language=en}}</ref>\n\nIn April 2018, Fitzpatrick said that President Trump should stop attacking the FBI and allow [[Robert Mueller]] to complete his investigation, stating it was improper to \"judge an institution based on the actions of a few bad actors.\"<ref>O'Malley, James; Rep. Brian Fitzpatrick to Donald Trump: 'Stop attacking the FBI'; Bucks County Courier Times; April 10, 2018; http://www.buckscountycouriertimes.com/news/20180410/rep-brian-fitzpatrick-to-donald-trump-stop-attacking-fbi</ref>\n\n====Health care====\nFitzpatrick opposed the [[American Health Care Act]], a bill to repeal and replace the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]]. In a statement, Fitzpatrick said, \"After considering the current healthcare bill in a thorough and deliberate manner, I have concluded that, although the American Health Care Act focuses on several much-needed reforms to our healthcare system, in its current form I cannot support this legislation\".<ref>http://www.phillyvoice.com/us-rep-fitzpatrick-i-cannot-support-republican-health-care-plan/</ref> Fitzpatrick joined many of his Republican colleagues as well as the majority of Democrats in an effort to oppose the bill.\n\nOn May 4, 2017, Fitzpatrick also voted against the second attempt to pass the American Health Care Act. In a statement, he said, \"We saw what happened when healthcare reform \u2013 an issue impacting 1/5 of our economy - was rushed through Congress along extremely partisan lines in 2009,\" referring to the ACA in 2010.<ref>http://www.buckslocalnews.com/news/bucks-county-congressman-brian-fitzpatrick-votes-against-american-health-care/article_adede5c6-3102-11e7-a1fb-8f1ba8a8ebef.html</ref>\n\n====Narcotics trafficking====\nFitzpatrick sponsored the International Narcotics Trafficking Emergency Response by Detecting Incoming Contraband with Technology (INTERDICT) Act, which was signed into law by President [[Donald Trump]] in January 2018. The new law directs $15 million to U.S. Customs and Border Patrol to expand screening for [[fentanyl]] and [[opioids]] at the U.S. border.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Boyle|first1=James|title=Trump signs Brian Fitzpatrick's fentanyl screening bill|url=http://www.buckscountycouriertimes.com/news/20180110/trump-signs-brian-fitzpatricks-fentanyl-screening-bill/1|accessdate=16 January 2018|publisher=Bucks County Courier Times|date=January 10, 2018}}</ref>\n\n====Gerrymandering====\nIn September 2017, Fitzpatrick urged the U.S. Supreme Court to limit extreme partisan gerrymandering in the Gill v. Whitford case. He stressed that partisan redistricting had undermined the Founding Father's vision of the House of Representatives remaining the voice of the people.<ref>Fitzpatrick-Led Brief Pushes Supreme Court on Redistricting Reform; Highbeam; September 6, 2017; https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-503574984.html</ref>\n\nHe was the only Republican Congressman from Pennsylvania who did not take part in a February 2018 lawsuit challenging a new district map drawn by Democrats. He explained that he opposes the drawing of congressional districts by elected officials of either party, saying instead that they should be drawn by independent, nonpartisan citizen panels.<ref>Otterbein, Holly; This Republican is bucking his party in the fight over Pa.'s new congressional map; The Inquirer; February 27, 2018; http://www.philly.com/philly/news/politics/rep-brian-fitzpatrick-fighting-gerrymandering-pennsylvania-20180227.html?arc404=true</ref>\n\n====Term limits and congressional perks====\nIn April 2018, Fitzpatrick led a bipartisan group of freshmen House members in an Oval Office meeting at which they discussed with President Trump a proposed constitutional amendment imposing congressional term limits.<ref>O'Malley, James; Fitzpatrick talks term limits with Trump; The Times; April 26, 2018; http://www.timesonline.com/news/20180426/fitzpatrick-talks-term-limits-with-trump</ref>\n\nIn May 2018, Fitzpatrick and [[Stephanie Murphy]] (D-FL) introduced H.R. 5946, the Fostering Accountability, Integrity, Trust, and Honor (FAITH) in Congress Act, that would \"end certain special perks reserved for Members of Congress, enact a lifetime ban preventing former Members of Congress from becoming lobbyists, and withhold Members' paychecks if they fail to pass a budget on time.\"<ref>Powers, Scott; Stephanie Murphy sponsors 'congressional accountability' bill; Florida Politics; May 24, 2018; http://floridapolitics.com/archives/264498-stephanie-murphy-sponsors-congressional-accountability-bill</ref>\n\n====Gun policy====\nIn 2018, Fitzpatrick was the only Republican to receive an endorsement from the [[Giffords Law Center to Prevent Gun Violence]], the gun control organization founded by former U.S. Representative [[Gabrielle Giffords]]. Fitzpatrick voted to expand background checks and to restrict assault weapon sales.
